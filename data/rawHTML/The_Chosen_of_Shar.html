From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

The Chosen of Shar is a sub-quest of both  Shadowheart's ⁠Companion quest and her ⁠Origin quest. It is added to the journal after encountering information on Dark Justiciars.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

The Dark Justiciars[edit section | visual editor]

During dialogue with  Shadowheart, she reveals she has always dreamed of becoming a Dark Justiciar of ⁠Shar, but the  Mother Superior thwarted any attempts at doing so. More information on the Dark Justiciars can be found at various places, starting in Act One. This starts the quest The Chosen of Shar to learn more about the Dark Justiciars and their history.

Information on the Dark Justiciars can be found within the Inner Sanctum of the Emerald Grove by examining the murals with Shadowheart in the party. One of the frescos, 'Fallen,' depicts a group of druids standing over a vanquished army - the army of Shar. The druids aided a group of Selunites in vanquishing Shar's army at Moonrise Towers.

If  Halsin is saved during ⁠Rescue the Druid Halsin, he can tell more about a war that was fought between the Selunites and the Dark Justiciars.

If travelling to ⁠through the Underdark and exploring Grymforge, several signs of Dark Justiciar remnants can be noticed. In the area near the Deep Rothé in the quest ⁠Get Past the Rubble, there are several such corpses which Shadowheart can comment on. Additionally, there is an  Idol of Shar at X: -645 Y: 366 which can be found on the path past the rubble, through many sets of pressure plate traps and a locked wrought iron gate. The gate can be smashed with force damage or passed with spells such as  Misty Step or  Gaseous Form.  Mage Hand can also be used to switch the lever on the other side of the gate. Speaking to Shadowheart, she can be gifted the statuette (with unique reactions for clerics of ⁠Selûne). Lastly, within the path to the Adamantine Forge, there are several  Devilfoil Masks which indicate that  Merregons from the hells fought with the Dark Justiciars, and a conversation with  Kith while investigating a demolished temple wall highlights the presence of sulphur crystals, suggesting that a  huge devil was responsible.

Unfortunately, the trail goes cold within Grymforge. Choosing to free  Nere as part of ⁠Free True Soul Nere results in his explaining that he was looking for an entrance to an ancient Sharran Temple, but the way was blocked. The temple is visible from just past the locked double doors where he was trapped, but the bridge leading there has been destroyed. It is not until the latter half of Act Two that the party can find a way in.

The Gauntlet of Shar[edit section | visual editor]

As part of ⁠Infiltrate Moonrise Towers, the party members are prompted to ⁠Find Ketheric Thorm's Relic, which leads them to the Grand Mausoleum. After solving a small puzzle in the Mausoleum, it is possible to enter the Gauntlet of Shar.

In order to reach the  Nightsong in the Shadowfell, the party must obtain four  Umbral Gems. Three of them are granted upon completing the Trials of Shar. The fourth one must be acquired from the orthon  Yurgir, also residing in the Gauntlet (see below). Once all four Umbral Gems are in the party's possession, if Shadowheart is in the party, the  Spear of Night must be obtained from the Silent Library to be able to deal with the Nightsong. If the player character twice refuses to search for the spear before going to the Shadowfell, Shadowheart becomes hostile to the party.

Shadowheart does not need to personally be taken through the Gauntlet of Shar, although she does have numerous opportunities for unique dialogue and approval.

The Shadowfell[edit section | visual editor]

To complete the quest The Chosen of Shar, Shadowheart must be taken to the Shadowfell. If not included, she immediately appears after the party returns to the material plane to confront them, saying they prevented her from achieving her destiny. She then permanently leaves the party, saying she hopes she never sees them again and forgets them in time. The quest The Chosen of Shar then fails.

Once leaping to the heart of the Shadowfell, it is revealed that the Nightsong is not an object, but an aasimar named  Aylin. She has been held captive using  Balthazar's necromancy by  Ketheric Thorm for over a century, to forcibly share her immortality with him.

After this, several events can play out, depending on whether Balthazar is alive and whether the player character convinces Shadowheart to spare Aylin.

Shadowheart can be convinced to spare Aylin in several ways, depending on her Approval rating with the player character and her Nightsong Points.

If Aylin is spared, she thanks the party after Shadowheart throws the  Spear of Night into the abyss of the Shadowfell. Aylin promises to tell Shadowheart more about her history after taking down Ketheric Thorm. Then, she flies off to face him. After leaving the Shadowfell, Shar subjects Shadowheart to great torment before casting her back to the mortal realm, and Shadowheart loses her favour. After completing ⁠Defeat Ketheric Thorm, and upon speaking to her in camp,  Aylin reveals that sometime after being freed, she retrieved the Spear of Night from the Shadowfell, altered it, and now gives it to Shadowheart in its new form as  Selûne's Spear of Night.

If Aylin is killed, Shadowheart becomes a Dark Justiciar and is rewarded with  Shar's Spear of Evening. However, the party lose a valuable ally in Aylin, and there are major consequences for the inhabitants of the Last Light Inn as a result of to Shadowheart's actions:  Isobel is overwhelmed and unable to sustain her protection magic over the Inn, causing all within to fall to the  Shadow Curse. Shadowheart refers to this as cleansing the resistance to Lady Shar's will. Additionally, unlike instances where Isobel is kidnapped in ⁠Resolve the Abduction,  Jaheira is nowhere to be found and does not aid the party in the fight at the Last Light Inn.

Regardless of Shadowheart's choice, one primary goal remains the same: Ketheric Thorm must be faced as part of ⁠Defeat Ketheric Thorm. This is either mandated by Shar, who sees him as a traitor, or urged by Aylin, who promises to reveal more information on Shadowheart's past if she assists.

Quest Rewards[edit section | visual editor]

If Shadowheart kills the Nightsong:

  • Dark Justiciar Boots
  • Dark Justiciar Gauntlets (Rare)
  • Dark Justiciar Half-Plate (Very Rare)
  • Shar's Spear of Evening

If Shadowheart spares the Nightsong:

  • Moonlight Glaive
  • Selûne's Spear of Night (granted after completing Infiltrate Moonrise Towers, upon speaking to Dame Aylin in camp)

Bugs[edit section | visual editor]

  • If Shadowheart has equipped the  Spear of Night upon going into the final cut-scene, it remains visible on her back in some shots even if she has already thrown it away.
