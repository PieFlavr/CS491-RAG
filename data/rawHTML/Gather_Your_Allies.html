"Gather Your Allies" redirects here. For the action this quest grants, see Gather Your Allies (class action).

Gather Your Allies is a main Quest spanning all three Acts of Baldur's Gate 3. It is obtained in Act One and completed in Act Three. Its objectives are fulfilled by completing various other quests throughout the campaign.

“
Thou art alone, but for thy friends. Didst thou not make allies? I fear thy final confrontation will prove quite a challenge. Hadst thou allies, the yoke of salvation may not have weighed down on thee so. But, since thou didst not convince others to join thy cause, thou wilt face the Netherbrain's assault armed only with thine own faculties. Let us hope they are enough, lest this plane fall to illithid ruin. I wish thee good fortune, and good grace.
„
— Withers, if the party failed to recruit any allies

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

Some objectives for this quest are mutually exclusive. For example, siding with  Nine-Fingers Keene during ⁠Aid the Underduke locks the party out of being able to ally with the Zhentarim in the same playthrough.

All Companions must be recruited in Act One and their personal story quests must be resolved for their objectives to be completed in Gather Your Allies.

Consequences[edit section | visual editor]

  • After completing ⁠Free Orpheus, the final battle begins to ⁠Confront the Elder Brain. The player character receives the special action  Gather Your Allies which summons friendly units or grants buffs during combat based on the characters or factions who have been recruited. The recruited characters also appear in a room in the High Hall, where they give a short dialogue line of support.

Notes and references[edit section | visual editor]

  1. ↑ If  Raphael is killed, the contract can still be given to Mol, but Raphael's death must not be mentioned, and a successful Persuasion Check must be passed. As a Warlock, a specific dialogue choice is in place of the skill check.
  2. ↑ Agreeing to Mizora's ultimatum is not necessary.
  3. ↑ Volo's passive buff,  Volo's Guide to Monsters, is also a ⁠manual for the Dungeons and Dragons tabletop game.
  4. ↑ If, during ⁠Kill Raphael's Old Enemy, the party complete the subquest ⁠Break Yurgir's Contract by defeating  Lyrthindor, Yurgir will automatically join them against  Raphael. If the party killed Yurgir or persuaded him to kill himself, he can still be convinced to assist them with a  DC 30 Persuasion Check. Either way, if he helps the party defeat Raphael, then he agrees to help in the final fight.
  5. ↑ Recruiting Zevlor may not be shown in the journal.
