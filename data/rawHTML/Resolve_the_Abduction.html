Resolve the Abduction

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Resolve the Abduction is a sub-quest of ⁠Infiltrate Moonrise Towers in Act Two of Baldur's Gate 3. It can be started by speaking with  Isobel at the Last Light Inn or by speaking to  Disciple Z'rell in Moonrise Towers.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

Meeting Isobel[edit section | visual editor]

After arriving at the Last Light Inn and gaining  Jaheira's trust, the party can ask Jaheira how to get past the shadow curse as part of ⁠Seek Protection from the Shadow Curse. Jaheira refers the party to  Isobel, a Cleric of ⁠Selûne - the woman responsible the Last Light Inn's special protection. Isobel can be found in the only room on the upper floor, where she is outside on the balcony, reinforcing the Inn's barrier with the power of the moon.

Isobel instantly recognizes the party as True Souls with free will and provides them with a  Blessing of Selûne. She explains that the blessing provides resistance to the lesser aspects of the curse, but is not enough to stave off the deepest parts of it. If the party betrayed  Kar'niss during ⁠Follow the Convoy and gave the  Moonlantern to the Harpers, Isobel gives the lantern back to the party. Otherwise, she mentions that the Harpers work on learning how the cultists are surviving the shadow curse.

Alternatively, if the party ventured to Moonrise Towers first during ⁠Follow the Convoy, the party can receive a mission from  Z'rell after completing ⁠Find Ketheric Thorm's Relic and helping  Balthazar to capture the  Nightsong. Z'rell congratulates the party for competing the mission but explains that there is one more task before she can grant an audience with  Ketheric Thorm; he seeks the safe return of his daughter, Isobel, and has sent a spy,  Marcus, to the Last Light Inn to bring her back to Moonrise Towers. Z'rell orders the party to aid the spy in capturing Isobel. After arriving at the Inn, the party can encounter Jaheira as normal and proceed upstairs to speak to Isobel.

Marcus arrives[edit section | visual editor]

After wrapping up with Isobel, she hears something strange. Suddenly, a Flaming Fist named Marcus flies down to the balcony. Marcus telepathically reveals that he was sent to capture Isobel alive. Passing  DC 6 Wisdom Check to probe further into his mind, reveals that the task is of great importance to Thorm. The party can reject him, or agree to help him. Isobel asks what is going on, and the party has an option to reveal Marcus' plan or state their intention to aid him.

Alternatively, if the tieflings were not saved during ⁠Save the Refugees, either by raiding the Druid Grove or ignoring the quest, the events play out slightly differently. When Jaheira confronts the party at the Last Light Inn's entrance, Marcus comes forth to vouch for them. He uses his telepathic powers to tell the party to follow his lead, revealing himself to be a True Soul spy. After the confrontation, Marcus can be spoken to in Isobel's room upstairs, where he explains that he was sent on a mission to kidnap the cleric.

The party can either attack him or go down and report it to Jaheira with passing a Persuasion or Deception check. If Jaheira is convinced of his betrayal, she tells the party to go to Isobel to protect her while she gathers her most trusted Harpers. However, the party can still choose to fight Marcus or aid him.

Battle at the Inn[edit section | visual editor]

Regardless of who the party side with, Marcus prepares for battle and summons his minions.

If the siding with Isobel, then the party have to fight against:

  •  Fist Marcus
  •  Winged Horrors - 6x

If the siding with Marcus, then the party have to fight against:

  •  Cerys
  •  Harper Bor
  •  Harper Essius
  •  Harper Naysa
  •  Harper Mijah
  •  Isobel
  •  Rolan

The party do not need to fight anyone beyond Isobel, where the goal is to knock her out with non-lethal attacks.

The Fallen Inn[edit section | visual editor]

If Isobel is captured or killed, the Inn falls. Everyone besides the party and Jaheira, who are protected by Selune's blessing, are either killed immediately or transformed by the shadow curse. The party has to face a massive battle against the shadow cursed residents:

  •  Dammon (if alive).
  •  Fist Ulthred
  •  Harper Antony
  •  Harper Branthos
  •  Harper Callie
  •  Harper Chenti
  •  Harper Darragh
  •  Harper Donner
  •  Harper Elifer
  •  Harper Elindale
  •  Harper Essius
  •  Harper Hangfar

Additionally, Shadow Creepers pop up to join the undead, eventually joined by a Giant Shadow Creeper.

After the battle[edit section | visual editor]

There are far reaching consequences depending on who was sided with. Regardless of the choice, characters with the Folk Hero background gain the inspiration ⁠Meeting of Fates.

Siding with Isobel and Jaheira[edit section | visual editor]

If siding with Isobel, and Marcus and the ghouls are defeated, she continues to sustain the warding over Last Light's Inn. Jaheira continues to operate from Last Light Inn until the party are ready to defeat Thorm once and for all. When ⁠Find Ketheric Thorm's Relic is complete, Jaheira and her Harpers start the assault on the Moonrise Towers. She can be invited to join as an Attached Follower until getting to the towertop and defeating Ketheric for the first time. Then she can be recruited as full Companion.

If siding with Isobel and Jaheira and Isobel is killed or kidnapped and Jaheira survives, then Jaheira states her intention to go to Baldur's Gate for more support. The party can either wish her luck on her journey, or suggest that she joins the party, to which she agrees.

Siding with Marcus[edit section | visual editor]

If Isobel is kidnapped, she appears at the end of Act Two during ⁠Defeat Ketheric Thorm. Once reaching the bottom of the Mind Flayer Colony and confronting Thorm for the last time, Isobel is there as well. She has been infected by a Mind Flayer Tadpole and enthralled with magic to keep her compliant. If the party aided Thorm in kidnapping her, she thanks them and says she was a fool to resist, then wishes them the best of luck in the ⁠City of Judgement. When the party fight Thorm, Isobel aids him. If the party manage to kill Thorm while leaving Isobel alive, she dies immediately after the battle.

Losing the Inn[edit section | visual editor]

Losing the Inn has many far-reaching consequences. If the party does not speak to  Counsellor Florrick on the ground floor, then she does not move on to Baldur's Gate and dies in the Inn.  Art Cullagh also dies, preventing the completion of ⁠Wake up Art Cullagh, although ⁠Lift the Shadow Curse can still be completed by the use of  Speak with Dead. If Dammon has not been spoken to,  Karlach's personal quest, ⁠The Hellion's Heart stalls since he can no longer fix her engine.

Losing the inn also grants the ⁠Absolute Devastation inspiration to party members with the  Soldier background.
