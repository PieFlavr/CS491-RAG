Feed the Mind Flayer

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Feed the Mind Flayer is a Quest in Act Three of Baldur's Gate 3. It can be started by speaking with the starving mind flayer in Rivington.

Objectives[edit section | visual editor]

Kill a humanoid and pick up the body.
  • A ravenous mind flayer asked us to bring it a humanoid to feast on.
Bring the body to the mind flayer.
  • We killed a humanoid for the mind flayer - hopefully this will sate its hunger.
Quest Complete
  • We defeated the mind flayer.
  • We convinced the starving mind flayer to hunt for its food.
  • We left without helping the mind flayer.
  • The now-sated mind flayer granted us a boon of psionic power.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

Screaming can be heard from the Abandoned Windmill in Rivington at X: 44 Y: -32. Lockpicking the old rusty door allows the party to investigate. A hatch leads to the windmill basement, with an unconscious  Mind Flayer within.

Interacting with the Mind Flayer provides the following options:

  • It doesn't feel very fortunate.
  • Wake the newborn mind flayer.
  • [BARBARIAN] WAKE UP, SQUIDFACE
  • [GREAT OLD ONE] Connect your mind to this fledging aberration - wake it up.
  • [PALADIN] This abomination's existence pollutes the very thin air - try to end it.
  • Attack the creature before it wakes up.
  • [STEALTH] Sneak away. (DC 10)

Choosing to attack the creature immediately starts combat with a level 8 mind flayer. Killing it provides a  Mind Flayer Brain, which can be consumed, as well as a Windmill Key,  Ring of Truthfulness, a random gemstone, and  Absolute Orders: Newborn. Consuming the brain grants one illithid potential.

Killing the mind flayer also earns the inspiration ⁠Kill 'Em While They're Young for party members with the Haunted One background.

If choosing to wake the newborn, the party can hear its demands. It explains it needs a fresh brain to feed on, and can be answered in several ways:

  • I'll see what I can do.
  • I can fetch you a corpse, freshly killed. Brain intact.
  • I never work for free.
  • Offer the mind flayer the body you're carrying. (if carrying a corpse)
  • [PERSUASION] Go find your own meal! Even weakened you're more powerful than anything out there. (DC 15)
  • [PALADIN] [DRUID] I will not let you feed on anything in this city, aberration.
  • I'm not going to bring you food.
  • Attack!

Suitable corpses[edit section | visual editor]

If agreeing to help, a corpse must be found; any will suffice, including from killing random characters.

Existing corpse options include:

  • A dead refugee near the Ironforge Gnome hideout at X: 45 Y: 66
  • A murdered refugee in the Well in front of Sword Coast Couriers
  •  Brilgor buried behind the Open Hand Temple at X: -106 Y: -19
  •  Father Lorgan in the Open Hand Temple at X: -57 Y: -22
  •  Ffion Goldgrind on the upper levels of Fraygo's Flophouse at X: -13 Y: 76

The party must pick up the corpse and bring it back to the Mind Flayer, to be rewarded with one skill point for the Illithid Powers tree and the following inspiration:

  • ⁠Newfound Subject ( Noble backgrounds)
  • ⁠Unexpected Parenting ( Haunted One backgrounds)

If fed, it leaves the windmill after a long rest. It later re-appears in Armbrust's Home in the Lower City, killing  Tym Armbrust and his family. The Mind Flayer can still be killed for its brain after giving it a corpse, but consuming it at this point does not grant an additional illithid skill point.

If persuaded to get its own meal, the mind flayer leaves to hunt with no further reward. This results in the death of nearby picnicker  Sloncha Owena and the disappearance of her beau  Bogdan Vortleson.

Quest rewards[edit section | visual editor]

Kill the mind flayer

  • Mind Flayer Brain (grants one point for the Illithid Powers skill tree if consumed, just like a  Mind Flayer Parasite Specimen)
  • Ring of Truthfulness
  • ⁠Kill 'Em While They're Young ( Haunted One backgrounds)

Feed the mind flayer

  • Ring of Truthfulness
  • One point for the Illithid Powers skill tree
  • ⁠Unexpected Parenting ( Haunted One backgrounds)
  • ⁠Newfound Subject ( Noble backgrounds)
  • Paladins break their oath.
