Investigate the Beach

From bg3.wiki

Investigate the Beach is a quest in Act One of Baldur's Gate 3. It can be initiated by approaching the Secluded Cove at the north-eastern region of the Emerald Grove.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

The Secluded Cove is north-east of the druidic ritual circle in the Emerald Grove. Upon approaching, strange singing can be heard at X: 271 Y: 523. Near the water, a child  Mirkon is enchanted by the singing of Harpies at X: 327 Y: 549. The party can try to focus on the singing or tell Mirkon to return to shore.

  • I don't hear anything. Come back to shore.
  • Focus and try to hear what he hears.
  • [ROGUE] [SLEIGHT OF HAND] Pick his pockets while he's distracted. (DC 5)

If the party focus on the singing, they can try to resist the song:

  • [WISDOM] Struggle against the music's pull. (DC 5)
  • [WISDOM] Struggle against the music's pull. (DC 5 ) (if passive DC 10 Nature roll passed)
  • [DRACONIC BLOODLINE] [CHARISMA] Foul creatures - mere carrion birds compared to your bloodline. Focus the will of the dragon against their song. (DC 5)
  • [SORCERER] [CHARISMA] Focus your will. Improvise a magical shell to dampen the music. (DC 5)
  • [BARD] [PERFORMANCE] Focus by singing: 'There once was a lady who met with a merrow, and tried to disclose it to her foolish fellow...' (DC 5)

If the party successfully resist the song, they can attempt to overpower or distract Mirkon:

  • [BARD] [PERFORMANCE] Focus by singing: 'There once was a lady who met with a merrow, and tried to disclose it to her foolish fellow...' (DC 5)
  • [BARD] [PERFORMANCE]  '... Her man would not listen, he thought it tall tales, until he rubbed up against all of her scales!'  (DC 10 )
  • [BERSERKER] [PERFORMANCE] ROAR. OVER. THE VOICE. (DC 10 )
  • [BARBARIAN] [PERFORMANCE] Drown out the harpies with a ROAR. (DC 10 )

Regardless of what he is told, Mirkon attempts to move closer to the harpies.

The harpies utilize a combination of the terrain and their  Luring Song to bring Mirkon and the party into close range. One harpy always remains at a distance to perform the song, causing party members to waste their turns if they fail to resist. Meanwhile, the other harpies fly close to attack the party. Most attempts to approach distant harpies are slowed because of the area's Difficult Terrain. However, pre-combat castings of  Enhanced Leap (in conjuntion with high Strength and  Feather Fall) as well as strategic high-ground positioning of melee characters may alleviate this issue. The harpies can  Fly over the distance and terrain, which allows them to easily  Multiattack.

Should melee combat be delayed or unavailable, ranged attacks and spells cast on the singing harpy can also be used to break its Concentration. If broken, another harpy likely sings, if not currently engaged in melee.

Additional preparation for combat may involve  blessing the party before speaking with Mirkon to improve resistance to Luring Song. Using gnomes, elves, or half-elven characters with Advantage on saves can improve the likelihood of resisting the song.

An aggressive yet viable strategy to protect Mirkon involves throwing or shoving him away from the shoreline and the harpies. Throwing him provides more distance from the enemies and may reduce his likelihood of being killed, even if damaged by the fall, which causes him to go prone for several turns.

Casting  Calm Emotions protects Mirkon and all party members within its radius from being affected by Luring Song, significantly reducing combat difficulty (the game log still shows a saving throw being rolled, but the characters are unaffected on failures).

Alternatively, the party can use  Silence and remain within the silenced area. In both cases, the party should be careful to prevent the caster's Concentration from being broken. If the party member casting Calm Emotions or Silence is a cleric, they may first cast  Sanctuary on themselves to prevent broken Concentration.

Casting Silence over Mirkon before speaking with him immediately triggers the fight, skipping all dialogue. If deciding on this strategy, it is advised to cast Silence not only over Mirkon but on the entire party, too.

Once the harpies are defeated, Mirkon informs the party that he needs to get back because  Mol will be mad at him. Before leaving, he tells the party to find  Doni and say him that they want to see the "Dragon's Lair".

If the party trigger this quest and then travel back to camp,[verify] Mirkon dies. He also dies if ⁠Save the Refugees is resolved by completing the Rite of Thorns or by evading the goblin attack.

Quest rewards[edit section | visual editor]

  • Access to the tiefling children's hideout, the "Dragon's Lair"
  • ⁠Protector of the Small ( Folk Hero background)
  • ⁠Slaying the Piper ( Urchin background)

Notes[edit section | visual editor]

  • This quest is triggered when crossing at X: 299 Y: 542; taking any long rest after it is triggered results in Mirkon's death.
  • Mirkon's corpse can be found near the harpies' spawn during ⁠Find Doni, if he is not saved prior.
  • Helping Mirkon is an easy way to gain favor with Mol and initiate ⁠Steal the Sacred Idol.
  • If Mirkon is killed by a third party during the battle with the harpies, the quest is bugged, both ending in success and teleporting a now-revived Mirkon away from the beach. This is easy to trigger by summoning Lump and his ogre band during the battle; they attack a Lured Mirkon, killing him, triggering the bug in the process.
  • Although he has no notable possessions, pickpocketing Mirkon while he is entranced grants ⁠The Allure of Coin for party members with the  Criminal background.
  • The bard's ability to use Performance against the harpies is a reference to the bard's ability in past editions called "Counter Song".
  • While talking to Mirkon, using Improvised Melee Weapon with another character and moving him away from the beach actually breaks him from the harpies' trance and completes the quest without having to fight them. The harpies are then revealed in the map afterwards.
