Find the Githyanki Crèche is a sub-Quest of the main quest of Act One, Find a Cure. It can be initiated as soon as  Lae'zel is rescued during ⁠Free Lae'zel if she joins the party. For the majority of Act One, this quest is directly connected to Lae'zel's Companion quest, ⁠The Githyanki Warrior.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

As part of ⁠Free Lae'zel, Lae'zel is trapped in a cage and speaks telephatically with the party to let her out. If asked why they should do that, she states that she has a cure for the tadpole, but she will only share it if they set her free. Two tieflings,  Nymessa and  Damays discuss how their friend  Zorru was nearly gutted by a squad of githyanki warriors. Assuming Lae'zel is with them, they intend to bring her back to the Druid Grove so that their leader can deal with her. The party can either kill them, or convince them to run away and let the party deal with Lae'zel.

Once Lae'zel is freed, she explains that the cure can be found at a githyanki crèche. They have a special device - a Zaith'isk - which can cure those infected by mind flayer tadpoles. Hearing that there is githyanki activity in the area, Lae'zel suggests they track down Zorru and see what he knows.

Zorru can be found in The Hollow, near the tiefling's storehouse. If the party speaks to him with Lae'zel in the party, he is instantly terrified that she has come to kill him. Regardless of the choices made, Zorru reveals what he knows if Lae'zel is around, as he's too scared to disobey.  Yul, one of his friends was gutted in front of him, his corpse can be found near the Mountain Pass. Another of Zorru's friends can be found at the Blighted Village, labeled as Dead Tiefling at X: 5 Y: 366. She is in the same room as the ogres. Using  Speak with Dead on her reveals that she too encountered githyanki in the mountains.

The Githyanki Patrol[edit section | visual editor]

See this transcript page for an in-depth exploration of this conversation and its consequences.
The githyanki patrol is west across the Risen Road at X: -101 Y: 555. They are arguing with a group of Flaming Fists. Suddenly, a red dragon comes down from the sky and sets the fists aflame. If Lae'zel is in the party, she notes that the crèche must be near and runs ahead to meet with the patrol, temporarily leaving the party. Go with her to start a conversation with the dragon's rider,  Kith'rak Voss.

This conversation is complex, with multiple variations depending on whether Voss speaks with Lae'zel, a gith origin, or any other character. Each variation requires a different number of ability checks to avoid hostilities. This page focus on the path of least resistance, with or without Lae'zel.

Lae'zel Speaks with Voss[edit section | visual editor]

If Lae'zel is in the party, the encounter becomes much simpler.

  • Nod to Lae'zel. Go ahead.
  • [GITHYANKI] I will be speaking on my kin's behalf, Kith'rak.
  • I will be speaking on her behalf, gith.

Choosing the first option allows Lae'zel to speak, and Voss reveals the search for a githyanki artefact. He asks if Lae'zel has seen anything like that. Choose the ability check:

  • [PERSUASION] Mouth a silent command to Lae'zel: 'play along'. (DC 10)

After the DC 10 check, another passive Deception check happens in the background using the player character’s Charisma (and deception proficiency if applicable). If Voss determines that Lae'ze is lying, he commands the patrol to attack.

If succeeding the check, Voss commands Lae'zel to go to the crèche and aid the search for the artefact before flying off.

A Gith (or "Gith") Origin Speaks with Voss[edit section | visual editor]

If a githyanki origin, or a character is disguised as gith using  Disguise Self, Voss asks what they are doing so far away from their crèche. If the character is accompanied by non-gith characters or those disguised as non-gith, he questions their company. If the player character is merely disguised as a gith, they must pass a  DC 15 Deception Check or  DC 15 Performance Check in order to earn the Kith’rak’s trust. This is also the case if the player character is actually a gith but has non-gith companions present. If a githyanki origin character is alone or accompanied by gith-only companions, there is no check.

If successful, he explains the search for the artefact and demand the party head to the crèche to join the hunt before flying away. Otherwise, the party must fight the patrol.

A Non-Gith Origin Character Speaks with Voss[edit section | visual editor]

This is the most difficult path. If Lae'zel is not in the party, or the player character insists on speaking on her behalf, Voss asks why he should not run them through. The player character must convince Voss not to kill them on the spot:

  • [SLEIGHT OF HAND] Silently hold his stare and discreetly prepare to attack. (DC 15)
  • [INSIGHT] You would've done so already if you didn't want something from me first. (DC 10)
  • [INTIMIDATION] I just want passage - if I have to go through you, so be it. (DC 15)
  • [BERSERKER] [INTIMIDATION] ROAR. (DC 18)
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] You'd sprain your wrist trying. (DC 18)
  • [PERSUASION] I don't know you. I pose no threat. (DC 15)
  • [DROW] Can't you muster a real threat, like flaying or blinding?
  • [RANGER] [FIGHTER] Try it - let's see who walks away alive.

...followed by a  DC 10 Arcana Check check to perceive his casting of  Detect Thoughts, then a  DC 10 Wisdom Check check to resist it. Wizards, Warlocks and Sorcerers can recognize the spell and bypass the Arcana check, and only need a  DC 5 Wisdom Check check to resist it. The interrogation can then end peacefully with a third successful check:

  • [ROGUE] A corpse is useless. But a living informant might unearth something.
  • [PERSUASION] I could help if you let me live - search for this weapon, perhaps. (DC 15)
  • [INTIMIDATION] Why are you hesitating, then? Try it, I dare you. (DC 15)
  • [PERSUASION] You ought to just *leave*. You've no right to decide my fate. (DC 15)
  • [OATH OF DEVOTION] [PERSUASION] Did you not feel the devotion when inside my head? I could be an invaluable ally. (DC 15)
  • [OATH OF THE ANCIENTS] [PERSUASION] You must've seen my knowledge of these ancient lands. I could be of use to you (DC 15)
  • [OATH OF VENGEANCE] [PERSUASION] You saw how ruthlessly I pursue my goals. I can help you. (DC 15)
  • [OATH OF THE CROWN] [PERSUASION] You will have seen my thoughts are disciplined. I make a valuable ally. (DC 15)
  • [OATHBREAKER] [PERSUASION] My fate is my own. You will not take it from me. (DC 15)
  • [BERSERKER] [INTIMIDATION] ROAR. (DC 15 )
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] Give it a try. I'm hungry for a little fun. (DC 15 )
  • [DETECT THOUGHTS] [WISDOM] Read his thoughts.
    • Don't be too hasty. I could prove very useful.

Passing of any of these checks results in Voss letting the party live, granting the ⁠Silver Blades, Silver Tongue inspiration to party members with the Charlatan background. He tells them to go to the crèche - ahead, at the Mountain Pass.

Otherwise, Voss becomes suspicious that the party is either infected with tadpoles or possessing the artefact, then he flies off on his dragon and orders his soldiers to kill them all.  Sarth Baretha,  Raider Chost,  Raider Zastri, and  Gish For'reth attack the party.

If the remaining members of the githyanki raiding party are killed, the ⁠Conquer the Conquerors inspiration is awarded to party members with the Soldier background.

Lastly, the party can find an  Elaborate Slate on the body of Sarth Baretha. If looted or pickpocketed from her, the party can attempt to decipher it:

  • [ARCANA] Focus on the object. Perhaps you can reveal its secrets. (DC 10)

Githyanki characters can successfully use the slate with no check. If Lae'zel is in the party, she can take the slate and decipher the slate immediately. Deciphering the slate reveals the location of the Githyanki crèche.

Accessing the Monastery[edit section | visual editor]

The Rosymorn Monastery is accessed from the Mountain Pass. The party must find the Cable Car Wheel at X: -47 Y: -74 and pass a  DC 15 Strength Check to summon the cable car. Then it is necessary to climb on the cable car and use the wheel to move towards the Monastery. If this does not work, there is an alternate path to reach the Monastery. Upon reaching the Monastery a cutscene triggers where a group of githyanki warriors shoot down some cultists.

If not engaging those warriors in combat, the Rosymorn Monastery Doors are locked and magically enchanted, but there are several routes inside. For instance, there are broken windows that can be climbed through at X: 64 Y: 31 west of the doors. Once inside, there are a some drunk Kobolds, who can be snuck past or killed. The party must move north to the giant crack in the wall. From here, it is neccessary to jump up the rocks to get higher up on the Monastery.

The Githyanki Crèche[edit section | visual editor]

From upper floor, the party must move north up the hall, jump across the gap to the east, and then move south, then east to find a set of crumbling stairs. After jumping past the gaps and following the stairs down, the party must move north to a barricade on the western wall, which must be destroyed to walk through. Moving north through the oak door, then west past the statue of Lathander, lets the party find a lever on the wall at X: 78 Y: 49, which opens the monastery doors; this makes reentering the monastery easier.

After moving west, and following the stairs to north, the party can reach the Monastery Basement at X: 74 Y: 80, where Crèche Y'llek can be entered. The githyanki are suspicious to outsiders, but a gith character (or disguised as such) or having Lae’zel in the party can allow them entry into the créche without a dialogue check. Alternatively the party can gain access by persuading, intimidating, or deceiving the guards.

The Infirmary[edit section | visual editor]

The infirmary is on the southwestern end of the crèche at X: 1299 Y: 809.  Ghustil Stornugoss is in charge of Zaith'isk purification and research on the illithids. Speaking to her and revealing that the party is infected with tadpoles initiates the purification.

Passing a  DC 15 Investigation Check, reveals that the Zaith'isk is a synthesis of illithid anatomy and metal alloys. It hums with psionic energy hinting at paths into unknown minds and unseen planes.

Origin Character Sits in the Zaith'isk[edit section | visual editor]

Stage one[edit section | visual editor]

If the player character sits on the zaith'isk, the device pulses and they feel utter agony, triggering an  DC 12 Intelligence Saving Throw to seek the tadpole:

  • [INTELLIGENCE] Follow the doctor's instruction. Seek the tadpole. (DC 12)
  • [GITHYANKI] [INTELLIGENCE] Follow the ghustil's instruction. Seek the tadpole. (DC 12)
  • [INTELLIGENCE] Focus the device on your tadpole. (DC 12) (if using it without Stornugoss)
Stage two[edit section | visual editor]

Unfortunately, the parasite only doubles down and burrows further, triggering  DC 15 Wisdom Saving Throw. The Netherese magic within the tadpoles causes it to fight back again:

  • [WISDOM] Stay calm. Guide the device closer. (DC 15)
  • [GITHYANKI] [WISDOM] Remember your orders. Bring the device closer. (DC 15)
Stage three[edit section | visual editor]

Finally, one last saving throw must be made:

  • [CONSTITUTION] Keep going. Complete the extraction. (DC 18)
  • [LAE'ZEL] [CONSTITUTION] Pursue the visions you saw. See where they lead. (DC 18)
  • [GITHYANKI] [CONSTITUTION] Complete your purification. Surrender to Vlaakith's will. (DC 18)
  • [MONK] [WISDOM] Channel your ki. Flood the creature with your essence. (DC 18)
  • [DRUID] [WISDOM] Seek the tadpole's essence. Flood it with power. (DC 18)
  • [CLERIC] [WISDOM] Pray for deliverance. Flood the creature with divine power. (DC 18)
  • [INTELLIGENCE] Focus on this ancient magic. Feed it with your own. (DC 18)
  • [WIZARD] [INTELLIGENCE] Study the ancient magic. Trace its forms. Align yourself with them. (DC 18)
  • [THE HEXBLADE] [CHARISMA] Beg your patron to aid the tadpole in its battle. (DC 18)
  • [ARCHFEY] [CHARISMA] Beseech your patron to lend the tadpole a mote of faerie arcana. (DC 18)
  • [FIEND] [CHARISMA] Ask your patron to lend the tadpole some diabolic might. (DC 18)
  • [GREAT OLD ONE] [CHARISMA] Entreat your patron - ask it to lend the tadpole eldritch power. (DC 18)
  • [BARD] [CHARISMA] Harmonise with the tadpole's magic. Amplify it. (DC 18)
  • [PALADIN] [CHARISMA] Share your oathbound magics with the tadpole. (DC 18)
  • [SORCERER] [CHARISMA] Follow the strange magic to its source. (DC 18)
  • [BARBARIAN] [CONSTITUTION] LET THE TADPOLE FEED ON YOUR RAGE. MAKE IT STRONG. (DC 18 )

Lae'zel Sits in the Zaith'isk[edit section | visual editor]

Lae'zel insists on going first. The player character can insist that they go first because Lae'zel owes her success to them.

  • [GITHYANKI] [PERSUASION] I have ably commanded this party. I will be cleansed first. (DC 10) (roll removed if high enough approval)
  • [PERSUASION] You would still be hunting for this place without my guidance. I will be cured first. (DC 10) (roll removed if high enough approval)
  • [INTIMIDATION] Out of my way. Or would you rather I split you in two and walk through the centre? (DC 10)
  • [GITHYANKI] [INTIMIDATION] Back off, or I will personally present your head to Vlaakith. (DC 10)
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] ROAR! (DC 10 )

Selecting the following option and sticking to it results in Lae'zel turning hostile against the party along with the Ghustil:

  • Make way, Lae'zel. I am purging myself of this damned parasite.

If telling Lae'zel to go ahead, she eagerly sits on the Zaith'isk, which grants  +10. If Lae'zel is convinced to leave the machine at any stage, be it by trickery or persuasion, the player character gains  +1  +1  +1  +1  +1  +1.

Stage one[edit section | visual editor]

If the player character passes a passive  DC 14 Perception Check check, they realize that the machine does not survive the procedure unscathed. The player character feels Lae'zel's mind ripping and rupturing and can persuade Lae'zel to jump out of the machine as soon as the procedure starts with a persuasion check:

  • [PERSUASION] Get out of there, Lae'zel. You won't survive this anguish. (DC 30)
  • [WISDOM] Call on your parasite. Show Lae'zel what she stands to lose. (DC 30)
  • [GITHYANKI] [HISTORY] Extract yourself. Mother Gith would not wish you to suffer. (DC 30)
  • Plead with the doctor. Lae'zel is in great pain.

Alternatively, if the player character fails the check or lets the procedure continue with the following options, this gives her the passive  Weakened Intelligence:

  • Endure, Lae'zel. the cure is worth the torment.
  • [GITHYANKI] Brace your mind, Lae'zel. Vlaakith tavki na'zin. Vlaakith tavki na'zin...
  • Wait and Watch.
Stage two[edit section | visual editor]

The player character sees a vision of past deceased githyanki and can try to convince Lae'zel to stop:

  • [WISDOM] Seek answers within the zaith'isk. What is this vision? (DC 21)
    • [PERSUASION] I've seen the truth Lae'zel. This device doesn't cure - it kills. (DC 21)
  • [GITHYANKI] [DECEPTION] Listen to me. I see your flesh pulsing. The zaith'isk will make you ghaik! (DC 30)
  • [DECEPTION] You must listen! The device is changing you. You are becoming illithid. (DC 30)

Otherwise, they can do nothing or urge her to continue which give her the passive  Weakened Wisdom:

  • Keep focused, Lae'zel. The cure is close.  +1
  • [GITHYANKI] Cry our queen's command. Lash'a'kla!  +1
  • Wait and watch.
Stage three[edit section | visual editor]

If Lae'zel is still in the machine, it is possible to persuade her to leave again. The difficulty is either 21 or 30, depending on the previous choices. Attempting to deceive her remains a  DC 30 Deception Check.

  • [CONSTITUTION] Pursue the visions you saw. See where they lead. (DC 21) (if visions weren't pursued in stage two)
  • [PERSUASION] I have seen the truth, Lae'zel. You will be killed, not cured! (DC 21) (if Perception check passed in stage two or the Constitution check)
  • [DECEPTION] Your tadpole is changing, growing. You have to resist the machine! (DC 30) (if Deception not tried yet)
  • [WISDOM] Focus your mind. Try to overload the device. (DC 30)

Failing the check, or allowing her to continue gives Lae'zel the passive  Weakened Constitution:

  • [GITHYANKI] Abide the pain, Lae'zel. It is Vlaakith's will!  +1
  • Embrace the pain, Lae'zel. Be pure.  +1
  • Wait and watch.

Alternatively, the party member can try to take control of the Zaith'isk and absorb its power to gain the  Awakened passive themselves:

  • [ARCANA] Draw on the zaith'isk's power, take it as your own. (DC 30)

Outcome[edit section | visual editor]

Even if all Saving Throws are succeeded, the Zaith'isk simply explodes. Stornugoss despairs at the loss of her life's work. If she thinks the parasite is still intact, she locks the party in the room and any attempt to escape causes the Infirmary to become hostile. The result is the same if the party wait for her to return; the occupants of the infirmary enter and become hostile. Despite the infirmary becoming hostile, the rest of the creche does not. If passing a  DC 15 Deception or Performance Check to throw her off, she then tells the party to leave.

If the crèche is hostile, the party must fight their way to the infirmary. The ghustil also fights the party, meaning she is not available to guide the party to using the Zhaith'isk. Despite this, the scene plays out nearly identically to if she was there, but without her comments or the need to deceive her at the end.

The Kith'rak[edit section | visual editor]

If  Kith'rak Therezzyn has not been spoken to as part of ⁠Discover the Artefact's Secrets, then Lae'zel demands the party speak to her. She believes that the ghustil is corrupt and tampered with the Zaith'isk. The party can go to her and ask to see the Inquisitor,  Ch'r'ai W'wargaz, as they believe someone in the crèche is a traitor. Regardless of what is said, she ignores it and says that she knows they have the artefact. She demands showing it to her. If refusing, she becomes hostile along with the other githyanki in the room. Otherwise, she locks the door and says that the party must speak to the Inquisitor.

Consequences[edit section | visual editor]

Using the zaith'isk can cause permanent debuffs to the character who entered it. Each failed saving throw results in a -2 to a stat. The first roll is wisdom, the second is intelligence, and the third is constitution. If Lae'zel goes into the zaith'isk, she also risks permanent debuffs. However, choosing to consume tadpoles eliminates the effect of the debuff.

A permanent buff known as Awakened can be gained by choosing "Draw on the zaith'isk's power, take it as your own" or succeeding all three saving throws and choosing any option except "Keep going. Complete the extraction." if the player character entered the zaith'isk.
