Free True Soul Nere

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Free True Soul Nere is a Quest in Act One of Baldur's Gate 3. It can be started when the party arrives at Grymforge and talks to  Sergeant Thrinn or after making a deal with Elder Brithvar.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

This quest is on a "timer", tracked by long rests or fast travels. The timer starts when speaking with  Nere by interacting with the rubble. After this, a warning in the form of a cutscene with Nere is triggered upon arriving in camp the first time after entering Grymforge. The quest can fail if long resting or fast travelling after it is started. The party can also let the timer run out and have Nere die prior to freeing him as an alternative way to solve the quest ⁠Deliver Nere's Head.

Arriving at Grymforge[edit section | visual editor]

The quest to free Nere is initiated the first time the party take the boat in the Decrepit Village to Grymforge. During the trip over, a group of duergar on another boat led by  Corsair Greymon questions the party on what they are doing on  Gekh Coal's boat:

  • [DECEPTION] I saw a body left by the myconids - your 'Gekh' must be dead. (DC 10) (if Gekh Coal unmet)
  • [DECEPTION] I'm afraid Gekh is dead - he fell fighting the myconids (DC 10) (if Gekh Coal was met)
  • [BRANDED] [PERSUASION] Just let me cross - I have the brand, see? (DC 5)
  • I work on Gekh's behalf. I'm after his sergeant's stolen boots.
  • I seek the sergeant - I believe she's keen to get these boots back.
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] MOVE YOUR BOAT, OR SINK. MAKE THE CHOICE. (DC 10 )
  • [INTIMIDATION] In the name of the Absolute - you will let me pass. (DC 10)
  • [ATHLETICS] Push the duergar into the water. (DC 15)

Succeeding the check results in the Corsair standing down and telling the party to speak with Sergeant Thrinn. If he is pushed into the water during the cut-scene his body irretrievably sinks to the botton of the water and a fight breaks out, earning  +5 and  +1 approval. The corpses of any characters shoved off of the boats during combat can be found washed up on the beach at the Decrepit Village afterward.

Once docked, another duergar,  Morghal, tries to extort money from the party. Telling her "I’m not giving you a single coin" results in her backing off immediately. The party must follow the path south to come across a cave-in, where a group of enslaved gnomes are being forced to chip away at the rocks at X: -635 Y: 311. Speaking to Sergeant Thrinn reveals that Nere, a drow and True Soul, is trapped by a cave-in.

Communicating with Nere[edit section | visual editor]

If interacting with the cave-in, Nere telepathically connects with the party and shows them what he sees. If passing a  DC 10 Perception Check to try to focus on what he can see, the interacting party member sees two gnomes on his side of the cave-in who are trying to remove rocks, as well as two dead gnomes who were killed by a magical trap. The player character also senses Nere's frustration and rage before the connection fades.

The cave-in[edit section | visual editor]

The primary objective of this quest is to destroy the caved-in wall of debris trapping Nere. It is an interactable object which has 44 Hit Points and is immune to all types of damage except for force (vulnerable), piercing (resistant) and bludgeoning. It also has the  Medium Toughness condition - a hit must deal 22 or more damage in order to damage it.

There several ways to destroy the cave-in. Due to its vulnerability to force, explosives such as  Smokepowder Satchel or  Smokepowder Bomb are viable solutions. However, sufficient force, or bludgeoning damage from abilities can also work - such as high rolls on a Warlock's  Eldritch Blast combined with the cave-in's force vulnerability, or melee attacks with high strength, Great Weapon Master: All In using a Maul. Finally,  Tavern Brawlers can use thrown weapons to destroy the cave in.

Placing any explosive object onto the floor (i.e. dropping it) should make all the digging gnomes scramble away, saving them from taking damage. However, after the rubble is cleared, Thrinn may run towards Nere. Should she take damage from any residual fire surface, the dialogue is skipped, and combat begins with all duergar and Nere. This risk can be mitigated by switching to turn-based mode, and targeting the fire surface with cold damage before Thrinn reaches it.[1]

The party can obtain enough explosives to complete this quest by solving the ⁠Find the Explosives quest, also located at Grymforge.

Successfully removing the caved-in rubble initiates a cutscene with Nere, which directly affects the quest ⁠Save the Grymforge Gnomes.

The mutiny[edit section | visual editor]

Upon being freed Nere is furious at being trapped and immediately takes it out on  Meerna who he casts into the lava.

If the party choose to say nothing or "Finish the slaves, True Soul. For the Absolute", then Nere massacres all the gnomes. If the party tell Nere not to kill innocents, he tells Thrinn to rip out their hearts and feed them to the Deep Rothé. At this point, if the party made a deal with Brithvar to kill Nere, he interjects saying that Nere owes the duergar money and he is going to get it from his corpse. Seething at this betrayal, Nere then says that he has changed his mind and will spare the party if they defeat Brithvar and the other mutineers.

If siding with Nere, the party must fight against:

  •  Brithvar
  •  Kur
  •  Morghal
  •  Orgarth
  •  Mirthis
  •  Danna
  •  Thudd

If siding with Brithvar, the party must fight against:

  •  Nere
  •  Elenna Thrinn
  •  Dalthar
  •  Drar
  •  Greymon
  •  Fonmara
  •  Dunnol

Siding with Nere[edit section | visual editor]

If Nere is sided with, there are two distinct paths which can be followed. One is where Nere tells the party to travel to  Ketheric Thorm at Moonrise Towers. The other is Nere being informed by the party that the Cult of the Absolute is a front for mind flayers, in which case he abandons the cult entirely.

After the battle, Nere says that the Absolute's business is unfinished. If selecting either "Unfortunate - but how does this concern me?" or "And what business is that, exactly?", he then bids the party to tell the General that he did what he could, but the route through Grymforge is impassable. If selecting "I'll do it. Where can I find the General?", Nere then explains that the party must travel through the Shadow-Cursed Lands. He had a Moonlantern which could ward off the shadow curse, but it was destroyed in the cave-in. If asking "If that's the case, how do I reach it safely?", Nere reveals there is a guide who will appear when playing the  Spider's Lyre he provides.

The divergence occurs based on what the party say next. If saying "I'd rather make my own way than rely on your help." then Nere says the Absolute should be demanding that he takes the party members' heads for such insolence, but he cannot hear her voice. Choosing the dialogue option "Explain that the Absolute is a front for mind flayers - you're protected from them." starts Nere on the path where he may falter from the Absolute:

  • [DECEPTION] It's true - it's a shadowy conspiracy headed by mind flayers and... tax collectors. (DC 10)
  • [PERSUASION] I was abducted by mind flayers. That was when I began to hear the Absolute. (DC 10)

Nere then needs one more push to abandon the cult:

  • [LOLTH-SWORN] Your faith in Lolth faltered. It is not too late to reclaim it.
  • [PERSUASION] You don't have to. Run - abandon the Absolute. (DC 10)
  • [HISTORY] People have followed cults for a long time. You're just another victim. (DC 10)
  • [DETECT THOUGHTS] [WISDOM] Cast Detect Thoughts
    • Your faith in Lolth faltered. It is not too late to reclaim it.

If convinced, Nere says dying for mind flayer filth is not worth it. He thanks the party for saving him from something worse than the duergar. After rewarding the party, he leaves and prays that he does not meet the party again.

Quest rewards[edit section | visual editor]

If Nere is sided with and abandons the cult:

  • Dagger +1

If Nere is sided with and remains in the cult:

  • Gift from the Absolute

If Brithvar is sided with:

  • 400 gold (or 500 gold if convinced earlier to give half the spoils)
  • ⁠Perfect Cut for party members with the  Criminal background

Loot from Nere if he is killed:

  • 1000 gold
  • Broken Moonlantern
  • Disintegrating Night Walkers
  • Mind Flayer Parasite Specimen
  • Nere's Head (conditional, see item page)
  • Sword of Screams

It is possible to get both  Gift from the Absolute and  Mind Flayer Parasite Specimen by first completing the quest then killing Nere later.

Bugs[edit section | visual editor]

  • When siding with Nere, just after the fight against Elder Brithvar, a conversation is supposed to begin automatically with Nere. However, a bug can occur during the fight, which causes the conversation to not trigger. This can be confirmed if the quest asks to talk with Nere, but in doing so, his response is "Leave me be. You've done enough." and nothing happens. As of patch 6, the bug is still present in the game. There is no known solution other than loading a save before the fight and redoing it.
  • If using an explosive that causes a fire surface near the rubble, and the rubble is not cleared from the first such explosion, the gnomes can walk over the fire surface and take damage; this provokes the start of a battle, as if the party had attacked the gnomes.
