Rescue the Druid Halsin

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Rescue the Druid Halsin is a sub-Quest of Act One's main quest, ⁠Find a Cure. It can be initiated after entering the Druid Grove by speaking with various Non-player characters about  Halsin's disappearance.

This quest is the same as ⁠Save the First Druid.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

Information on Halsin can be found by speaking to Nettie during ⁠Get Help from Healer Nettie or by speaking to Rath. Both Rath and Nettie are in the Inner Sanctum, which is accessible via the stone door north of the Sacred Pool. Mentioning to Rath that Kagha seems dangerous makes him lament that a snake has replaced their leader. Pressing for more information results in Rath revealing that Halsin disappeared at the goblin camp. Nettie discusses more about Halsin if the party is honest with her about their affliction and symptoms, stating that Halsin could better treat them if he was around.

Halsin can be found in the Worg Pens area at X: 393 Y: -60 of the Shattered Sanctum, in the interior area of the Goblin Camp. While in his wildshape bear form, his goblin captors are harassing him, unaware of his identity. Details on how to approach the Goblin Camp and free Halsin are described below.

This quest can also be completed successfully by initiating the quest ⁠Raid the Grove. To accomplish this, the party must reveal the location of the Emerald Grove to  Minthara, causing her to lead an assault on the grove. While besieging the grove, the party can betray her. This strategy lures a good portion of the goblin forces from the Goblin Camp to the grove, causing Halsin to free himself and return to the Grove, once the siege defence is successful.

Entering the Goblin Camp[edit section | visual editor]

There are various check-point encounters on the path to the Goblin Camp. There are several methods to approach these, including but not limited to:

  • A frontal assault.
    • This approach may lead to turning the entire camp hostile if an alarm drum is successfully sounded.
  • A mixture of diplomacy checks, such as Persuasion and Deception.
    • Alternatively, it is possible to brute force through all diplomacy checks by utilizing the Tadpole Power during dialogue.
    • Drow, or characters disguised as one via the  Disguise Self spell, have special dialogue options to bypass diplomacy checks.
  • Avoiding the check-points through Stealth or finding an alternative path (i.e. approaching the camp from the north-west).
    • Approaching from the north-western path requires the party to deal with some sleeping guards who can be woken accidentally if a jumping party member lands near them. A  Silence spell can effectively prevent this.

Upon successfully clearing or bypassing the check-points and entering the camp proper, it is possible to freely partake in Goblin Camp activities, converse with the partying goblins, and take on various side quests (more information on the Goblin Camp page). Specifically for this quest, the party must venture into the interior of the temple.

The Shattered Sanctum[edit section | visual editor]

There are three entrances into the Shattered Sanctum - the Heavy Oak Doors at X: 105 Y: 454, the Passageway at X: 51 Y: 476, or through the Cracked Wall on the upper level of the camp at X: -106 Y: 481.

The Heavy Oak Doors:

  • This entrance is guarded by  Warrior Trinzas, who can be persuaded through diplomacy, Tadpole Powers, or drow specific dialogue like the previous check-points.
  • Violence is always a viable alternative, however, there is a risk the fighting draws the attention of other goblins in the area, leading to the alarms being sounded and all the goblins turning hostile.

The Passageway:

  • The path to this entrance is "guarded" by three Blast Mines (two charged with Poison Cloud and one with Explosion). Entering by this way places the party at the southeast corner of the Shattered Sanctum.

The Cracked Wall:

  • The wall is an object with 22 Hit Points, Sturdy, and immunity to all damage types except piercing (resistant) and force (vulnerable).
  • This makes force-damage items/spells/abilities that can clear the 10 damage threshold - like  Smokepowder Bombs or  Eldritch Blast - ideal for breaking this wall.
  • There are sleeping guards situation close to the wall. If using the  Silence spell on the explosion or the guards to cover the noise, a confrontation can be avoided.

There is technically a fourth entrance to the temple from the Selûnite Outpost in the Underdark, but that door is inaccessible externally, and must be be opened from inside of the Shattered Sanctum first via the Stone Discs or the wall Lever.

Freeing Halsin[edit section | visual editor]

Once inside, the party must approach the ornate door at the eastern side of the Shattered Sanctum at X: 342 Y: 32 to enter the Worg Pens, where Halsin is held prisoner. As long as the alarm was not raised, no one stops the party, assuming they are either a True Soul or a welcomed visitor.

Alternatively, a party member can also get taken to the Worg Pens by getting themselves captured - either by offending the goblins (i.e. getting caught pick-pocketing), or through the potential events of the ⁠Ask the Goblin Priestess for Help quest (drinking Gut's potion of sleep while not being resistant to it). In this case, the party member is taken to the prison at the Worg Pens, and has the option to break out and find Halsin that way.

Regardless, once in the Worg Pen, approaching the cages triggers a cutscene where the goblins harass Halsin, who is disguised in his bear wildshape. Using  Speak with Animals allows the interacting party member to understand Halsin in his animal form. There are various dialogue options to react to the scenario. Regardless of what happens, in order to free Halsin, the goblins guarding the area must be fought, including  Butcher Vrak and  Beastmaster Zurk.

At the start of the fight, the goblin youths that were harassing Halsin try to flee and call for assistance. If successful, they draw in the guards outside the doors -  Sharp-eye Neem,  Sharp-eye Eef, and  Sharp-eye Nass.

Once freed, Halsin explains he came here with a group of adventurers to learn more about the mind flayer tadpoles, but was captured. He believes the goblin leaders are planning to assault the grove and offers to join the party as a temporary companion to escape. Accepting his offer turns the entire camp hostile. If Halsin's offer is rejected, he remains in the Worg Pens until the leaders are taken care of.

Priestess Gut[edit section | visual editor]

 Priestess Gut can be found in front of the giant throne immediately north of the front entrance at X: 297 Y: -18. She offers to brand the party with the Brand of the Absolute. Regardless of whether the brand is accepted, Gut recognises the party as fellow True Souls. She attempts to probe into the party's minds with her illithid powers. Allowing her to do this causes her to notice something is amiss. Explaining that it is the result of the tadpoles and asking if she can help, commences ⁠Ask the Goblin Priestess for Help. Gut says that the party should join her in her private chambers.

Following her to her chambers, she says that she has a cure for the tadpole and a special cleansing potion must be taken first. However, an Arcana check reveals that it is just a sleeping potion. If called out on this, or if the party member in question is resistant to sleeping potions (i.e. a drow, elf or half-elf), she becomes hostile, triggering several nearby goblins to do the same. However, if the potion is taken, she chains up the party member and says that she intends for them to become her pet squid. It is possible to break free with an Acrobatics or Strength check (Rogues do not need a check to break free). If nothing is done at all, a special cutscene is triggered where  Korrilla appears from a portal and assassinates both Gut and her Ogre guard. She then disappears after a brief conversation, warning the party to be more careful.

Minthara[edit section | visual editor]

The drow  Minthara is in the northeastern corner of the Shattered Sanctum at X: 333 Y: 42. There is a  Scrying Eye which must be dealt with before defeating her; otherwise, the entire camp is alerted. Minthara can easily be taken out after speaking with her, as she runs across the bridge to gather the troops. While she is running on the bridge, attack the bridge's posts and she falls into the chasm below. Otherwise, once the scrying eye is dealt with, only Minthara and her minions in the room become hostile, so the other two leaders can still be dealt with stealthily. If Minthara is only  Knocked Out instead of killed, she later can be recruited to the party in Act Two.

Dror Ragzlin[edit section | visual editor]

 Dror Ragzlin is unique because he is the only member of the trio who cannot be killed without alerting the two other leaders and the rest of the goblins; killing him, no matter how, turns the entire Shattered Sanctum and Goblin Camp hostile. For this reason, it is best to wrap up all business in the Goblin Camp and acquire the waypoint in the Selûnite Outpost in the [Underdark]] below the Shattered Sanctum before killing or knocking him out.

Dror Ragzlin is in the throne room to the north, casting  Speak with Dead on an illithid corpse at X: 296 Y: 40. A cutscene automatically plays as the party enter the room, with Ragzlin asking if they would like to speak to a squid.

If Ragzlin communes with the illithid without party interference, its killer is revealed to be the party after the second question. This can be avoided by taking control of Ragzlin's questions with a  DC 10 Wisdom Check and asking questions other than who is the killer such as "Why was the Nautiloid in Faerûn?" Asking an obvious question like "Who is the Absolute?" causes Ragzlin to be suspicious of the party. Otherwise, if the party is exposed as the illithid's killer, they can talk Ragzlin down through a Persuasion check or by using  Detect Thoughts to reveal that he likes being called the Absolute's Right Hand.

Back in the Worg Pens[edit section | visual editor]

Halsin is grateful if the party return and report on the leaders' defeat, and is then willing to answer many questions about his knowledge of tadpoles, as well as the strange movements of Absolutists at Moonrise Towers. He tells the party to return to the Emerald Grove where he will meet them again. Upon returning to the Sacred Pool area, Halsin confronts  Kagha about her actions. He either demotes her status to an initiate or casts her out entirely, depending on whether ⁠Investigate Kagha was completed.

Getting the Rewards[edit section | visual editor]

If speaking to Rath in the Inner Sanctum at X: 463 Y: -30, he rewards the party for their service. In the Enclave Library there is a giant stone wolf statue in the room. There are four plaques surrounding the wolf. The party must combine the rune with the empty socket at X: -463 Y: 24. After interacting with all four runes simultaneously, a hidden staircase is revealed, which leads to a special treasure room.

Notes[edit section | visual editor]

  • Once Dror Ragzlin is killed, the entire goblin camp, inside and out, becomes hostile. If the party spared Sazza from Minthara, Sazza may voice disapproval but does not become hostile. Thus, the She Cannot Be Caged! achievement can still be achieved.
  • Traveling to Rosymorn Monastery Trail and back causes Halsin to die, concluding this quest. This happens even if the party avoid any long rests during the transition, either forced or voluntary.
  • If having travelled to the Rosymorn Monastery Trail before doing a long rest, the celebration does not trigger. Then  Halsin can be found waiting at the Sacred Pool, where the party can talk to him; this works after talking to  Art Cullagh in the Last Light Inn in Act Two. The "Celebrate at camp" objective is skipped in this case.[verify]
