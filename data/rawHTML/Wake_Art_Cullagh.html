Wake Art Cullagh

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Wake Up Art Cullagh is a Quest in Act Two of Baldur's Gate 3. It can be started by speaking with  Fist J'ehlar at the Last Light Inn or by talking to Art.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

Meeting Art Cullagh[edit section | visual editor]

 Art Cullagh can be found in the Last Light Inn, in a bed on the ground floor in a room to the north. If the quest ⁠Rescue the Grand Duke was completed, a group of Flaming Fists are in the room, as well as  Counsellor Florrick and  J'ehlar. Florrick explains that when  Isobel cleansed the Inn, Art suddenly appeared. All attempts to talk to him failed, but he did have on him his original writ of duty, signed by ⁠Eltan, the founder of the Flaming Fists. Cullagh is looked after by Fist  J'ehlar, who explains that he has been completely unresponsive, but the party are welcome to try and speak with him. If the quest ⁠Rescue the Grand Duke has not been completed, they can talk to Art directly to begin the quest.[1]

Cullagh repeats the same song:

“
"Thaniel and me are climb, climb, climbing up a tree.

We, we see what we see and do just what we please, together waiting for the sun forever.
We see shadows, they get darker, but our hiding place is brighter.
Monsters snuffling and stalking, in the shade where we are walking.
We are fearsome, black and red. We are living, they are dead.

Two of us safe 'til the end."
„
— Art Cullagh

Party members can try and observe him with several checks, each offering clues as to what happened to him:

  • [MEDICINE] Observe his present condition. (DC 14)
  • [CLERIC] [WISDOM] Try to commune with the man's soul. (DC 14)
  • [ARCANA] Search for signs of magical influence. (DC 14)
  • [SORCERER] [ARCANA] Look for any signs of magic afflicting this man. (DC 14 )
  • [SORCERER] [ARCANA] Recall your studies... What could be causing this? (DC 14 )
  • [HISTORY] Try to identify the song. (DC 14)
  • [BARD] Hum the tune he was singing - try to identify it.
  • [BARD] Examine his body for clues as to a preferred instrument.
  • [DETECT THOUGHTS] [INTELLIGENCE] Probe his mind.
    • Repeat what you heard in his mind: 'Thaniel'.

Passing one or more of these checks results in the party noticing his mind was gripped by the Shadowfell, but not broken by it. They can also notice that his hands have calluses, like someone who played a stringed instrument:

  • [BARD] Search his body for any indication of a preferred instrument.
  • [BARD] He seems musically-inclined. Look him over for any related clues.
  • [INVESTIGATION] Look closer at him. There may be something you missed. (DC 10)

J'ehlar also produces a Worn Letter which was in his pocket. The letter indicates Cullagh was tasked with investigating the House of Healing, which was suspected of being corrupted. The party can ask J'ehlar if she has heard the song before, or if she knows who  Thaniel is, but she has no idea on either front. If the quest ⁠Rescue the Grand Duke has not been completed and a party member passes a perception check, the letter will appear on the bed next to Art.

Halsin's aid[edit section | visual editor]

There is one person who is intimately familiar with the Shadow-Cursed Lands, though -  Halsin. If the Druid Grove was protected during ⁠Save the Refugees, Halsin is at camp and, as one of the few living people who was around the area before the curse, admits to knowing much about the land. If the party speak to Halsin and ask him about Thaniel, he says he knows the name well and immediately sets off to examine the unconscious Fist. He tells the party to meet with him at the Inn. Once there, Halsin says there is no way Cullagh could know Thaniel's name without meeting him and that it is imperative he is woken up. He suggests finding something to trigger his consciousness, like a memory or an item.

The House of Healing[edit section | visual editor]

Cullagh's writ of duty references the House of Healing, making it the first stop in finding a way to awaken him. If the party enter through the front door, they are stopped by  Sister Sinda, who says the party must wait in line until the doctor is available. The party can persuade, intimidate, or use a performance to convince Sinda that they desperately need to see the doctor immediately. The  Dark Urge origin and  Astarion (if he is chosen to talk as a companion, or is an origin as well) have unique dialogue options to be let in, which results with Sinda admitting the party without any checks. Sinda can also be bypassed by entering the hospital building from multiple other points of entrance, and moving to the operating room. Once they reach it, this line of dialogue with her is not activated anymore.

Once inside the House of Healing at X: -199 Y: -16, the party must move north to the operating room. Here,  Malus Thorm is operating on a distressed "patient"[2] by torturing them. He stops to tell the party that they are welcome to learn from him.[3]

  • I don’t wish to study, only to lift the curse on this land.
  • A student, yes. Do please enlighten me.
  • You will stop this sick spectacle at once.
  • [DRUID] I am no student, I seek knowledge of the curse. It is a blight on this land.
  • [WIZARD] I am a scholar, this is true - just not in the study of torture.
  • What wrong has this man done to deserve such torture?
  • [BARBARIAN] Just kill them already - all this torture is pointless.
  • [ROGUE] In my experience, torture is usually more pain and less proselytising.
  • Attack.

If the party attack Malus outright, he is joined by Sister  Hunna, Sister  Vanessa, Sister  Anya, and Sister  Geanne. Otherwise, Malus explains they have nearly cured the "patient." He states that absence is the heart of ⁠Shar's teachings, and asks "What is the light of eyes but the cancer that causes one to witness the laceration of being?" With this pronouncement, he tears out the patient's eyes, completing the "cure." He then asks the party if they are ready to be cured as well.

  • [INVESTIGATION] Examine the sisters and their implements. (DC 14)
  • [ROGUE] [INVESTIGATION] Cast your gaze over the blades of the sisters. (DC 14 )
  • [RELIGION] Recall what you know of Shar’s teachings. (DC 14)
  • [CLERIC] [RELIGION] Recall what you know of Shar’s blasphemous teachings. (DC 14) (if not a Cleric of Shar)
  • [PERSUASION] The sisters aren’t ready. They’ll make me sick instead of curing me. (DC 16)
  • [PALADIN] You are the cause of the only suffering here - I am duty bound to end you.

A successful Investigation check reveals the sisters' blades are bloodied and dull, and that only the most measured hand could make a clean incision. Passing a Religion check lets the party recall a Sharran maxim: "Go forth and sow doubt, but do not compel it - for only the willing may know the Lady's embrace." With the former, the party can suggest the sisters practice on each other. With the latter, the party can suggest that the sisters practice on Malus himself. A unique option is made available if the party sneaks behind Malus before the cutscene and moves to the room to the north. Inside is a book  Surgery and Physiology: A Sharran's Primer at X: -199 Y: 77 which explains that Malus has a 'final lesson' of loss.

  • [INTIMIDATION] I have read your treatise. Initiate the final lesson, or die by my hand. (DC 18)
  • [PERSUASION] ‘Only with sacrifice is mastery gained.’ For the sisters to learn, you must submit. (DC 18)
  • [FIGHTER] [PERSUASION] Sparring is vital to progress. Have them practice on each other. (DC 21 )
  • [ROGUE] [PERSUASION] Practice - that's how I got good with a blade. They should spar each other. (DC 21 )
  • [WIZARD] [PERSUASION] In my years of study, I have found no better teacher than example. Perhaps you could volunteer? (DC 21 )
  • [ROGUE] [PERSUASION] Practice - that's how I got good with a blade. They should spar each other. (DC 21 )
  • [MONK] [PERSUASION] When the fist trembles, sometimes another is needed to steady it. A good master would volunteer themselves. (DC 21 )
  • [BARD] [PERSUASION] I take joy in performing with a master of my craft - I'm sure the sisters are the same. Why don't you volunteer? (DC 21 )
  • [PERSUASION] Why not have them hone their skills on each other? (DC 21)
  • [PERSUASION] They need a better subject to practise on first. Not a student, but a master. (DC 21)
  • [BARBARIAN] I suffer from boredom. The cure is ripping you apart.

Once Malus is dealt with, the  Battered Lute can be looted from his corpse. This special lute with the initials AC meticulously carved into the body must be played at Art Cullagh's bed in Last Light Inn to wake him up and grant one or more of the following inspirations:[4]

  • ⁠Favour for a Favour ( Noble background)
  • ⁠Strings of Fate ( Entertainer background)
  • ⁠Waking the Sleeper ( Sage background)

He immediately asks for Thaniel, and says Thaniel said to find Halsin.

If Halsin is still alive he is eager to track down Thaniel, progressing ⁠Lift the Shadow Curse. He asks the party to meet him by the lakeside to commence this quest. Otherwise, if Halsin is dead, the quest ends without resolution and the region remains locked under the effects of the shadow curse.

Notes[edit section | visual editor]

  1. ↑ If Art Cullagh dies due to Isobel being killed or kidnapped during ⁠Resolve the Abduction, it is possible to speak to him with  Speak with Dead to find out more about Thaniel. This can also be used to proceed with ⁠Lift the Shadow Curse.
  2. ↑ If the Distressed Patient is not killed in the conversation with  Malus Thorm and survives the ensuing battle, he can be freed from his restraints. He then groans in pain and flees in terror.
  3. ↑ As a Cleric of ⁠Shar,  Shadowheart has unique dialogue if she speaks with Malus.
  4. ↑ If the party did not acquire the Battered Lute, playing any lute is sufficient to wake Cullagh and grant one or more of the mentioned inspirations.
