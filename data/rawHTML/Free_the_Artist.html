Free the Artist

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Free the Artist is a Quest in Act One and Act Three of Baldur's Gate 3. It is started by speaking with  Oskar Fevras at the Zhentarim Basement in Act One and does not resume until Act Three in Baldur's Gate.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

The Zhentarim Hideout[edit section | visual editor]

During Act One, to find Oskar, the Zhentarim Hideout must first be located. It is in a barn west of Waukeen's Rest, at the shabby door at X: -113 Y: 613. The party are automatically aware of the Zhentarim Hideout depending on how ⁠Find the Missing Shipment was dealt with. If the party undertakes this quest and manages to spare Rugan's life, he shares the location of the hideout along with the password need to enter. With a successful perception check, the party notice a man,  Salazon, crouching behind some shelves. Reciting the password "Little serpent, long shadow" has him stand down immediately and provide the key to a wardrobe which leads into the hideout. If the party does not have the password, there are a myriad of other options to get past him:

  • Read his mind.
  • [BERSERKER] [INTIMIDATION] Do NOT make me angry! (DC 15 )
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] Careful. You won't kill me, but you will make me mad. (DC 15)
  • [PERSUASION] Just calm down - I'm not here to fight. (DC 15)
  • [ROGUE] [PERSUASION] This is all pretty crude, as traps go - you'll probably kill us both. (DC 15 )
  • [SORCERER] [PERSUASION] I am magic. Cast that spell - you'll only hurt yourself. (DC 15 )
  • [DRACONIC SORCERER] [PERSUASION] Fire is in my blood. Do it. Let's see who survives. (DC 15)
  • [WILD MAGIC] [PERSUASION] Every spell I cast could be my last. You think I fear death? (DC 15)
  • [WIZARD] [PERSUASION] That spell, in close quarters? You're going to hurt yourself. (DC 15 )

Succeeding to make Salazon stand down allows the Zhentarim to be dealt with peacefully. Enter the Zhentarim Basement and follow the path down to encounter  Brem at X: 295 Y: -250. Brem is a trader for the Zhentarim and is holding an artist,  Oskar Fevras, hostage. He offers to have Oskar paint a portrait - for a fee. Speaking to Brem before Oskar provides unique dialogue options to persuade him that Oskar is more trouble than he is worth.

If choosing "A 'pet artist'?" to ask Brem more about Oskar, he explains they found him wandering the wilds, and that he needed protection. Choosing 'Protection'. It sounds a bit more like kidnapping. unlocks a dialogue about letting Oskar go:

  • Fine, I'll buy him. How much?
  • [BERSERKER] [INTIMIDATION] ROAR. (DC 20 )
  • [INTIMIDATION] We can skip threats and go straight to blades, if you like. (DC 20)  +1  +1  +1  +1  +1
  • [PERSUASION] If he's as talented as you say, someone will be looking for him. (DC 20)  +1  +1  +1  +1  +1
  • [ROGUE] [PERSUASION] Art's more valuable once the creator is dead. I can arrange that (DC 15 )  +1  +1  +1  +1  +1
  • [BARD] [PERSUASION] If this artist were to tragically expire, his portfolio would become finite - therefore priceless. (DC 15 )
  • [PALADIN] [PERSUASION] Slavekeeping's bad for business with any halfway-moral customer. Let him go. (DC 15)
  • [GITHYANKI] [INTIMIDATION] Here's my offer: give me your slave, or become mine. (DC 15 )  +1  +1  +1  +1  +1

Choosing "Fine, I'll buy him. How much?" causes Brem to name a price: 1,000 gold. This price can be knocked down to 600 gold with a successful  DC 15 Persuasion Check or  DC 20 Intimidation Check.

If speaking to Oskar to inform him of the good news, more information can be gleaned about Oskar - he is engaged to a wealthy woman but conflicted about leaving behind his first love. He asks for more gold for the road, stating his fiancé can repay the party handsomely for rescuing him. Regardless of paying up, he successfully escapes and returns to Baldur's Gate, granting the following inspiration:

  • ⁠An Artist in Need ( Guild Artisan background)
  • ⁠The Price of Ink ( Entertainer background)

Lady Jannath's Estate[edit section | visual editor]

Once in Act Three, the party can attempt to meet with Oskar in Baldur's Gate. Oskar can be sought out within the Lower City at Lady Jannath's Estate at X: -254 Y: -76. A butler named  Tarhun Mnemonis guards the door. Tarhun explains that  Lady Jannath sent away all the servants on vacation, including him. As he is on vacation, he cannot stop anyone from entering the estate, though he does warn against it. While the conversation is going on, screaming and banging can be heard from within the mansion.

On entering the mansion through the front door, it becomes apparent something ghostly is occurring: chairs and various objects are floating and, as the party climbs the stairs, they start to attack. Use dashes and jumps to reach Lady Jannath and a possessed Oskar, who are in a bedroom behind double doors on the third floor at X: -251 Y: -55 (second floor if coming from the rear entrance).

After a short cutscene, three Poltergeists appear to attack the party.  Knocking out Oskar immediately kills the three poltergeists. Killing Oscar causes Jannath to immediately kick the party out and prematurely end the quest. If the party cannot knock Oskar out and instead kill the poltergeists manually, they turn invisible.  Volo's Ersatz Eye and other sources of  See Invisibility can help in this encounter.  Faerie Fire and  Spirit Guardians are also helpful.

Once the poltergeists are dealt with, Jannath pleads with the party to help her. She needs discretion, which is why she sent the servants away. She notes there appears to be something off about the atelier at the top floor and directs the party to investigate there.

If going to the atelier the party must deal with a number of cursed skulls. The skulls deal thunder damage and knock back the party. Using  Silence can stop the skulls from casting their spells. The party can then use  Remove Curse or use throw a bludgeoning weapon with the thrown property at them, which destroys them in one attack. Also  Spirit Guardians can be useful to be rid of the skulls.

Once in the atelier at X: -268 Y: -52, a perception check reveals a strange wall. The wall can be accessed by a trick with Oskar's portraits. Taking the portrait of Oskar that is leaning against the western wall and then combining it with the empty easel opens up an additional section of the room and the possessed portrait within. There is also a locked chest behind the portrait. Lockpick the chest (DC 20) and read the  Letter to Oskar Fevras within. The letter reveals Oskar was corresponding with someone named  Mystic Carrion who was helping him with his ghostly problems.

Mystic Carrion's Mansion[edit section | visual editor]

The party must venture to Philgrave's Mansion at X: -8 Y: -158 to find Mystic Carrion. The western wall can be destroyed, lockpicked, or the passphrase from Oskar's letter can be used to open it.

Talking to Mystic Carrion about Oskar, lets Mystic Carrion remember dealing with him. He offers a solution - the  Torch of Revocation - for the price of 3,000 gold. He is, however, willing to waive the fee if the party agrees to find his missing servant,  Thrumbo, as part of ⁠Find Mystic Carrion's Servant. If Mystic Carrion is ultimately killed, the Torch can be found in a gilded chest behind where he was originally encountered at X: 18 Y: -162.

Returning to Lady Jannath's Estate[edit section | visual editor]

However the Torch is acquired, the party must bring it back to the atelier and equip it. The torch must then be used on the possessed portrait, if it is equipped as a torch, an off-hand melee attack must be performed to free the spirit of  Kerri Evenfield. Doing so grants an inspiration:

  • ⁠Flames of Passion ( Guild Artisan backgrounds)
  • ⁠True Exorcism ( Acolyte backgrounds)
  • ⁠Using the Proper Means ( Sage backgrounds)

Kerri is enraged that Oskar trapped her, and wants revenge. However, she can be persuaded or intimidated into backing down from killing Oskar:

  • [INTIMIDATION] I don't care if you're a spirit. Touch him, and I'll break you. (DC 15)
  • [DRUID] [INTIMIDATION] Touch him, and you'll face all of nature's fury. You'll face me. (DC 15 )
  • [RANGER] [INTIMIDATION] You're on the Material Plane - my hunting ground. Back off, or face me. (DC 15 )
  • [WILD MAGIC] [INTIMIDATION] I'll burn you, and potentially everyone else here, to ash if you don't stop this. (DC 15 )
  • [SORCERER] [WIZARD] [WILD MAGIC] [INTIMIDATION] You will burn like everything else on this plane, spirit, if you harm this man. (DC 15 )
  • [EVIL CLERIC] [INTIMIDATION] Stop this, or I will educate you on the true meaning of torment and suffering. (DC 15 )
  • [PERSUASION] The Kerri Oskar spoke of was kind and gentle. This isn't the real you. (DC 15)
  • [GOOD CLERIC] [NEUTRAL CLERIC] [PERSUASION] Your spirit is twisted by hate and fear, but this isn't you. You're kind and gentle - I can sense it. (DC 15 )
  • [MONK] [PERSUASION] Ask yourself - will killing this man make you happy? Give you purpose? Or are you better than this? (DC 15 )

If passing a check, she is then confused and starts to wonder why she is even there in the first place.

  • [DECEPTION] You came to deliver a message. To tell us what led to your fate. (DC 18)
  • [BARD] [ROGUE] [DECEPTION] You had a message for us, remember? You were going to tell us why you died by your own hand. (DC 18 )
  • [INTIMIDATION] You owe Oskar an explanation. And you're going to give it to him... (DC 18)
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] Tell Oskar what he wants to hear. OUT WITH IT. (DC 18 )
  • [GOOD CLERIC] [NEUTRAL CLERIC] [PERSUASION] Do not be shackled by your story, spirit. Share it - let it set you free. (DC 18)
  • [PERSUASION] No more harm will come to you by sharing your story. But it could help Oskar. (DC 18)
  • [OATHBREAKER] [PERSUASION] I won't let your soul be bound unjustly, spirit. Tell us of your fate, and you will be freed. (DC 18)
  • [PALADIN] [PERSUASION] Calm, spirit. Tell us of your fate, then your soul shall be freed - I swear it. (DC 18)

Otherwise, she attacks the party with two Vengeful Souls and two Tormented Souls. After defeating her and the souls, all the invisible Poltergeists throughout the house die and the ⁠The Throes of Love is granted to party members with the  Entertainer background.

If saving Oskar by fighting Kerri then Lady Jannath is upset about Oskar putting them in danger. The party can choose to help Oskar or tell Lady Jannath the truth.

If both Oskar and his marriage are saved, he is immensely grateful and offers to paint a  Personal Portrait as a reward. He goes up to the atelier and starts painting whenever the party is ready. He also allows the title to be chosen by the party, which does not affect the price of the portrait. [See: Bugs] Obtaining the portrait grants the ⁠Painted Hero inspiration to party members with the  Folk Hero background.

Quest rewards[edit section | visual editor]

  •  Personal Portrait painted by Oskar Fevras for saving both him and his marriage, which can be sold for 1500 gold.
  •  Till Death Do Us Part and  After Death Do Us Part for fighting Kerri and telling Jannath the truth; Oskar leaves and drop After Death Do Us Part on the floor just outside the bedroom. This may be an advisable option; see bugs for details.
  • Leaving Oskar to Kerri also rewards After Death Do Us Part.

Notes[edit section | visual editor]

  • The party do not need to pay the additional 200 gold when freeing Oskar for him to show up in Baldur's Gate and continue the questline.
  • The party may also choose to continue this quest if they chose to buy Oskar and treat him as a slave, resulting in him fleeing the party on bad terms. This all seems to be forgotten by Oscar when the party reach Baldur's Gate, and the quest continues as normal, though it is not updated anymore when going through its further stages.
  • If a romanced companion is in the active party when Oskar paints a portrait of the player character, they comment on how attractive it makes the player character look.
  • It is possible to obtain multiple paintings by talking to Oskar with different characters.
  • The bugged nature of  After Death Do Us Part makes it a very useful item. As such, it may be advisable to fight Kerri, and then tell Jannath the truth, to obtain it.

Bugs[edit section | visual editor]

  • Prior to Patch 7, the portrait painted by Oskar would not always have been that of the party member who spoke with him.
  • The poltergeists in Lady Jannath’s mansion can behave erratically, skipping multiple turns in a row without taking any actions.
  • The party can visit Mystic Carrion and ask for assistance with Oskar's condition before they visit Lady Jannath's Estate, despite having no way to know about him becoming possessed.
