Our Fiery Friend is the Companion Quest for  Karlach. It is automatically added to the journal when she joins the party in Act One.

Act One[edit section | visual editor]

Meeting Karlach[edit section | visual editor]

Karlach can be encountered as part of  Wyll's story or by speaking to  Anders in the Toll House in the quest ⁠Hunt the Devil. She can also be encountered on The Risen Road at X: 111 Y: 506. Karlach is below the bridge of the Toll Road, near the stream. Here, she appears to be struggling to control herself, her body engulfed in flames.

Appoaching Karlach nearby her spot triggers a cinematic which can play out in a few different variations. If the party raided the Grove or caused the conflict between Druids and Tieflings to erupt with Dammon being one of the casualties, Karlach becomes hostile upon the meeting and unavailable to recuit. The player character can try to persuade her to stand down or attack.

If the Grove's occupants remain unharmed, Karlach will be available for recruitment. Upon approaching her she mentions seeing the player character on the Mindflayers ship. If Wyll is in the party he starts the confrontation stating she is the "The stink of Avernus. Advocatus diaboli". In both scenarios after short exchange of words, the party and Karlach enter mind-meld tadpole connection showing first the vision of her experiences in Blood War in Avernus, then second vision of her slicing through Zariel's servants while desperately seeking escape.

Choosing to believe Karlach results in her joining the party. She asks for help - her former master, the archdevil Zariel, sent a group of demons after her who are masquerading as Paladins of Tyr. They are holed up in the Toll House, recovering from wounds from their first confrontation with Karlach. Karlach asks that she be brought along to confront them. If Karlach is brought, Anders is shocked and afraid. He says the paladins will not survive another assault and begs the party not to let her hurt them. A  DC 10 Insight Check reveals that there is a squint in his eyes which indicates he is lying. Using  Detect Thoughts also reveals that Anders serves Zariel. If Anders is called out on his lie, he quickly drops the act and reveals his true allegiance with Zariel, attacking the party. Once the battle is over, Karlach goes into a frenzy, destroying anything near and setting fire to the Toll House. Karlach explains that she has an Infernal Engine for a heart which lets her burn as hot as the Hells. Unfortunately, it has been running in overdrive ever since she left Avernus. She says she needs to find a mechanic that can tune up her engine and that the paladins let slip that a tiefling infernal mechanic is in the area.

Finding an Infernal Mechanic[edit section | visual editor]

The mechanic in question is a tiefling named  Dammon. When the city of Elturel fell to Avernus, Dammon was there and was put to work as a mechanic. Upon meeting Karlach, he instantly recognizes her heart is from the Hells. He believes he can help her, but he needs materials to do so. He has spotted several pieces of  Infernal Iron on his travels and believes this is what he needs to aid Karlach.

It is possible to catch up with Dammon in three places:

  • At the Druid Grove, in The Hollow
  • At the Last Light Inn, in the barn
  • In the Lower City, at the Forge of the Nine

There are many occasions where Dammon can die. During the raid on the Grove in ⁠Raid the Grove, Dammon can become a casualty. He also can die during an attack on the Last Light Inn by  Fist Marcus and his minions. If Dammon makes it to Baldur's Gate, he is safe, so long as the party do not randomly kill him. Additionally, Dammon can die if the  Strange Ox is killed in Act One, as it is later implied to have saved him on the road.

Infernal Iron Locations[edit section | visual editor]

There are several places to find Infernal Iron. Karlach's heart only needs two in total to get the full set of upgrades from Dammon. Possible locations include:

  • In the basement of the Blacksmith's House in the Blighted Village - inside the same chest as the  Blacksmith's Note
  • In the locked room above  Dror Ragzlin's throne in the Shattered Sanctum
  • Inside a chest located in a locked cell in the Zhentarim Basement
  • As a gift from  Kith in Grymforge if all three checks are passed about the Ruins
  • In the Mason's Guild in Reithwin Town in the Shadow-Cursed Lands

Upgrading Karlach's Heart[edit section | visual editor]

In order to repair Karlach's heart, the party must return to Dammon with Karlach, as she must install the upgrades herself.

After the first upgrade, Karlach still feels hot, but not as badly as before. She feels more powerful than ever, but this upgrade is not a long term solution. Dammon commits to keep working and says that he considers the possibility of enchanted coolant. He advises the party to keep an eye out for more Infernal Iron on the road for when they meet again.

Once the second upgrade is complete, Karlach is able to touch other people again without burning them up. She is immensely excited with this and can then be hugged. The moment is highly significant for Karlach, who has not touched another person in ten years.

If the first upgrade is done in The Grove, Dammon says that he needs to think further on how he can help Karlach. He does not have a clear idea until after the party meets up with him again in the Last Light Inn in Act Two. If the party initially encounter Dammon at the Last Light Inn, both upgrades can be received after a Long Rest. If Dammon is met in Baldur's Gate, then both upgrades can be received at the Forge of the Nine after a Long Rest.

Act Two[edit section | visual editor]

Moonrise Towers[edit section | visual editor]

At the end of Act Two, Karlach finds a new enemy if she is brought to the Mind Flayer Colony. The party realizes the Chosen Three are controlling an Elder Brain using three Netherstones and a Netherse crown. These individuals are  Ketheric Thorm,  Orin the Red, and  Enver Gortash. Karlach is shocked to see Gortash there, and even more so that he is calling himself the "Edict of Bane."

Later, Karlach explains Gortash was the reason she was sent to the Hells. Karlach grew up in Baldur's Gate, struggling to survive as a child. She had a rough childhood, brawling her way through the city. Gortash set his sights on her at a young age, and hired her on as a bodyguard. She initially trusted him deeply and was willing to put her life on the line to protect his. However, he betrayed her and sold her into the service of Zariel. Zariel had wanted to test her new Infernal Engine and Gortash said Karlach could handle it. Now that she is aware he is at the heart of the Absolutist plot, she vows to put an end to his misdeeds.

Act Three[edit section | visual editor]

Confronting Gortash[edit section | visual editor]

After entering Wyrm's Rock Fortress, Karlach finally has a chance to see Gortash in the flesh. Gortash takes control of a Steel Watcher and invites the party to his coronation. If Karlach is in the party, she prepares to confront him then and there. The party can react to this:

  • [PERSUASION] Careful, Karlach - the Steel Watch will destroy us in one swipe if we attack here. (DC 18)
  • [PERSUASION] We might need Gortash alive to save the city. Let's wait to strike until we've had the chance to question him. (DC 18)
  • [WYLL] Keep calm. If we do battle, my father will perish at our own hands.
  • Wyll is right. We attack Gortash, Ravengard falls too. (if Wyll is present)
  • I couldn't stand to see the Steel Watch get their hands on you. Let's wait for a more opportune moment. (if the player romanced Karlach)
  • Lead the charge, Karlach - Gortash dies today.

If the last option is chosen, Karlach attempts to run up to Gortash, but is stopped by a Steel Watcher. She is taken to Wyrm's Rock Prison, where the party can later release her.

“
Is that it, then? I've killed the bastard that ruined my life, and now I crawl into a corner and die?
Am I fucking missing something?
„
— Karlach, after killing Gortash
As part of ⁠Get Gortash's Netherstone, the party must eventually confront Gortash. If the party kill him for his Netherstone, Karlach wishes to see his dead body. Upon seeing the death of the man who sold her into servitude, she angrily laments his betrayal of her and the imminent loss of her own life and freedom.

Karlach's Fate[edit section | visual editor]

During the confrontation of ⁠Confront the Elder Brain, the party fail to control the Elder Brain, as it has become a Netherbrain.  The Emperor arrives and whisks the party to the safety of the Astral Plane and explains that only an Illithid can hope the defeat the Brain at this point. If the party do not trust the Emperor or side with  Orpheus, then Karlach can volunteer to become a mind flayer. If this happens, Karlach remarks that her engine feels quiet and she is able to stay in the mortal plane.

If Karlach does not transform into a mind flayer, her engine finally overheats after the battle is over. If Wyll becomes the Blade of Avernus as part of his story quest, ⁠The Blade of Frontiers, then he offers to go with Karlach to Avernus to try and find a solution to her engine problem. A character with a suitably high approval can offer to go with her, either alone or as a trio with Wyll. Otherwise, Karlach's desire not to go back to the Hells can be honored, in which case her engine fully overheats and she explodes into flames.

Leaving the Party[edit section | visual editor]

There are some instances in which Karlach permanently leaves the party. During Act One, if the party side with the goblins as part of ⁠Raid the Grove, Karlach immediately leaves the party. If the party raids the Grove without recruiting Karlach, then Karlach says she knows what they did. Enraged, she asks the player character if it made them feel big to abuse the refugees. She then becomes hostile and attacks the party.

If the party travel to the Rosymorn Monastery Trail or the Shadow-Cursed Lands before dealing with  Anders, Karlach announces she knows a bad situation when she sees one and permanently leaves.

Conflict with Wyll[edit section | visual editor]

As part of his mission as a devil hunter, Wyll is pact-bound to kill Karlach. With the aid of the tadpole's telepathic powers, however, Wyll can be shown that Karlach was a victim of the Blood War, rather than a perpetrator. As Wyll is further convinced, he realizes he has made a grave error and stands down.

In the conflict, rather than attempting to broker peace, the party can also pick a side with either Karlach or Wyll, causing the other side to become hostile. In this case, the hostile party member is lost, as they could not make amends.

If peace is brokered between Wyll and Karlach, the two become close. Several long rests after their fight,  Mizora, Wyll's patron, comes to punish him for failing to abide by their pact. As punishment, Mizora transforms Wyll's appearance, giving him horns and a more devilish appearance. After this, Karlach feels an immense guilt towards Wyll for having him sacrifice so much to keep her alive. Through this, they gain a strong bond of friendship.
