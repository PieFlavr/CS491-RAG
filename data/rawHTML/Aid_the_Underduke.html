Aid the Underduke

From bg3.wiki
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Aid the Underduke is a Quest in Act Three of Baldur's Gate 3. It can be started by speaking with  Farlin in Rivington or by visiting Sharess' Caress and listening to  Earspoon and  Borgus Elamin.

Help Jaheira find Minsc.

Search for the Stone Lord.
  • Jaheira has left the party. Nine-Fingers still wants Minsc - the Stone Lord - dealt with.
Find the Stone Lord at the Counting House.

  • Two drunken Guild members let slip that Nine-Fingers plans to ambush the Stone Lord at the city's Counting House. We need to get there first if we want to save him.
  • We met the guildmaster Nine-Fingers Keene. She's set a trap for her new rival, the Stone Lord. He's to be ambushed in the city's Counting House. She asked us to head over and make sure it all goes as planned.
Deal with the Stone Lord at the Counting House.
  • The lower levels of the Counting House are off-limits to the public. We need a vault pass from Head Clerk Meadhoney to enter.
  • Head Clerk Meadhoney revealed that the Stone Lord was taken down into the vaults by the Head Banker a short time ago.
  • The lowest level of the Counting House is protected by a great vault door. We need to enter a code to pass.
  • We found a book of significant dwarven dates in the Head Banker's office. A certain entry was circled - 1356.
  • The Stone Lord - Minsc - escaped Nine-Fingers' trap and teleported away. We should search the vault for clues on where he went.
Find the Stone Lord in the Sewers.
  • A cultist in the Counting House carried a note mentioning some hideout in the 'Cistern'. We should head down to the Sewers to search for the Stone Lord.
  • A dead cultist in the vault mentioned some hideout underneath the city. We should head down to the sewers to search for the Stone Lord.

Search the Sewers for the Stone Lord.

Walkthrough[edit section | visual editor]

The Guild and the Stone Lord[edit section | visual editor]

The party can learn of the conflict between the  Stone Lord and the Guild in two places in Rivington: on the Western Beach and in Sharess' Caress.

Rivington Beach[edit section | visual editor]

On the Western Beach near Rivington, west of the South Span Checkpoint, the party can encounter members of the Guild led by  Farlin, fighting a band of thugs serving the Stone Lord lead by  Cairos at X: -220 Y: 94.

Standing back and watching or choosing a neutral path, results in supporting the Guild. As a Bard, suggesting that the conflict be resolved by a round of flyting causes both sides to become hostile. If the members of the Guild are aided and survive, Farlin gives a reward of 674 gold afterwards. Additionally, one  Mind Flayer Parasite Specimen can be found atop a barrel at X: -225 Y: 107 and two more can be found on the ship at X: -214 Y: 131.

Overall, the choice of which side to join does not impact the later stages of this quest.

Sharess' Caress[edit section | visual editor]

Within Sharess' Caress, there are two men drinking on the ground floor,  Earspoon and  Borgus Elamin at X: 30 Y: 87. They are speaking about  Nine-Fingers Keene. The party can either listen in ( DC 15 Perception Check) or state "Talking about Nine-Fingers? I've got business with her." ( DC 10 Deception Check) The men talk a little more about the Guild's affairs, before questioning why the party members are still there:

  • [PERSUASION] Sounds like an interesting business. I'd like to take part. (DC 18)
  • [PERSUASION] I bet a few coins would loosen your tongue. (DC 15)
  • [DWARF] [PERSUASION] Come on, level with me. I'm not about to sell out another dwarf. (DC 15)
  • [ROGUE] [PERSUASION] I'm looking to join the Guild. How about you point me the way? (DC 18 )
  • [FIGHTER] [PERSUASION] I need work, and it sounds like Nine-Fingers needs steel. (DC 18 )
  • [DETECT THOUGHTS] [WISDOM] Read the dwarf's mind.
    • I need to talk to Nine-Fingers about Zenovia.

If one check is passed, they refer the party to the Guildhall, stating "Lower City. Basilisk Gate. Guildhall."

Entering the Guildhall[edit section | visual editor]

There are several paths to the Guildhall.

One route is to use the manhole at X: 127 Y: -30 in the alley north of the Basilisk Gate waypoint and smash through the western wall at X: 38 Y: 784. Following the path west leads to a locked door ( DC 20 Sleight of Hand Check) to the Guildhall.

Alternatively, above ground, there is an alley at X: 57 Y: -101 guarded by  Tusgront and several other Guild members. The party can attempt to enter the Guildhall by passing an ability check:

  • [DECEPTION] I came by earlier and lost something. I just want to retrieve it. (DC 20)
  • [INTIMIDATION] Are those your final words? Usually people scream for their mothers. (DC 20)
  • [HALF-ORC] [INTIMIDATION] Let me through, or I swear on Gruumsh's eye I'll tear your limbs from their sockets. (DC 20 )
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] Thank you for the list - now I know which parts of you to break first. (DC 20)
  • [PERSUASION] Surely one little peep won't hurt? It can be our little secret. (DC 20)
  • Careful - that's High Harper Jaheira you're speaking to. (if Jaheira present)
  • [DETECT THOUGHTS] [INTELLIGENCE] Read the muscle-bound half-orc's mind.
    • I've come from Darkhold. I'm here to join the Zhentarim inside.

If Farlin is aided in Rivington, Tusgront instead instantly recognizes the party as allies of the Guild and allows them to enter.

If a passive  DC 7 Perception Check check to notice the ring he is wearing was successful, the party can make a History check to recall the ring's meaning:

  • [HISTORY] Look closer at the ring. Try to recall the symbol's meaning. (DC 15)
  • [ROGUE] [HISTORY] Look closer at the ring. Try to recall the symbol's meaning. (DC 7)

This unlocks several more options to pass Tusgront:

  • [ROGUE] [DECEPTION] You don't recognise me? I'm Nine-Fingers' right hand. She needs me immediately. (DC 15 )
  • [DECEPTION] Hate to cut short conversation with such a handsome fellow, but I have urgent business with Nine-Fingers... (DC 15)
  • [INTIMIDATION] I'm here to see Nine-Fingers. Get in my way, and you'll lose more than your ring finger. (DC 15)
  • [HALF-ORC] [INTIMIDATION] Take me to Nine-Fingers, or I swear on Gruumsh's eye, I'll tear you limb from limb. (DC 15 )
  • [BARBARIAN] [INTIMIDATION] I'm Nine Fingers new muscle. Let me pass, or I'l show you why I was hired. (DC 15 )
  • Careful, that's the High Harper Jaheira you're speaking to. (if Jaheira present)

Keene is in her office, well aware of the Harper's entrance into her territory. She informs the party that she and her guild are searching for  Minsc of Rashemen, and he is going by the new title of the Stone Lord. He now works for the Absolute and is decimating the members of the Guild. For that reason, she intends to take him down. If Jaheira is in the party, she refuses to let the party confront Minsc themselves. However, there are plenty of other Guild members with loose lips.

Nine Fingers Keene's Plans[edit section | visual editor]

  •  Uktar at X: -30 Y: 754 reveals the plan if "The Quicker I deal with the Stone Lord, the quicker Nine-Fingers gets back to business" is selected
  •  Tut Thistlebog at X: -14 Y: 740 drunkenly gives away the information, if Jaheira is in the party or if his companion is properly persuaded.
  •  From the Desk of Rakath Glitterbeard, Head Banker in Keene's room at X: -37 Y: 763 can be read, which gives away her plans.

The Guild intends to ambush Minsc at the Counting House.

Entering the Counting House[edit section | visual editor]

The Counting House is one of the most heavily secured banks in Baldur's Gate and a challenge to infiltrate. Several characters in the building hint that something unusual is happening and causing a service backlog that day. The party can speak to the Head Clerk  Bloris Meadhoney at X: -103 Y: -179 and, make him through a combination of dialogue options,  Detect Thoughts and Insight checks reveal that the Head Banker,  Rakath Glitterbeard has taken the Stone Lord into the vaults under highly irregular circumstances. Passing a Persuasion, Intimidation, or Deception convinces Meadhoney to write the party a bank pass. If  Jaheira is present she automatically handles dialogue up to the ability checks and lowers the DC needed to pass.

Both the bank pass and the code to get further into the vault can be pilfered by sneaking upstairs. The bank pass sits on a side table in the corner of the first room on the left at X: -103 Y: -185. In the second room the party can find the book  Flintdigger's Abridged Dwarven History at X: -93 Y: -181. A sneaky rogue can attempt to sneak past the guards, or there is a pipe that connects the two rooms that a wildshaped druid can pass through.

After gaining a bank pass, the party can present it to Cashguard  Captain Faril who is guarding the way forward near Meadhoney. Down the stairs and to the east is another checkpoint, easily passed with the bank pass. There are a number of traps that are automatically disabled if showing a proper pass. Moving forward, the party can enter the vaults.

Once inside the vaults, the deepest area of the room is straight ahead. However, the door is locked and can be opened by a code. Entering the passcode to open the door by walking over four of the nine pressure plates in a correct order. Reading  Flintdigger's Abridged Dwarven History (in the Head Banker's office on the top floor, at X: -93 Y: -181) hints at the password being 1-3-5-6. There is a set of nine keys. By stepping on the correct four keys one at a time, the party can move forward. These keys are the top left, top right, the center key, and the middle right key. Alternatively, the code can be bypassed by creating an electrified water surface directly in front of the vault door, which causes it to open. This can be accomplished by using  Create Water or throwing  Water on the floor, then dealing ⁠⁠Lightning damage on the wet surface. The door can also be  lockpicked with a critical success.

If moving forward, the party can finally see Minsc in the flesh. Unfortunately, he disappears before he can be confronted. A group of Bhaalists attack the party as a parting gift. Once the Bhaalists have been defeated, the party must search for clues on where Minsc went. Approach the large vault at the end of the room at X: -691 Y: 873, which was completely smashed by Minsc, and a successful perception check shows mud from the sewers.

Into the Sewers[edit section | visual editor]

There are two possible ways to find Minsc: belowground and aboveground.

Belowground, Minsc can be found in the Abandoned Cistern. The party must jump the pipes above the caustic water at X: -186 Y: 883 and defeat the Bhaalists guarding the area. Going north, there is an area with a Sluice Valve, which is stuck. It can be unstuck with a successful athletic check and open up the door. Otherwise, it is necessary to interact with the temperature valve and the water level valve, timing it properly to open the door. The water level valve must be hit first, then after five seconds the temperature valve must be hit, too. That should open the door. Then, the party must walk through the sluice tunnel to find Minsc.

Aboveground, there is a garden area north of the Steel Watch Foundry with a door which says "Keep Out." The party has to enter it. There are two pressure plates on the east and west walls of the room. A party member must step on each of them at the same time to enter a secret area. It is also possible to place boxes on the switches instead. Once the door is open, there is a Blast Mine right through the doorway ( DC 15 Perception Check to spot it and  DC 10 Sleight of Hand Check to disarm it). In this room there is an ornate hatch that leads to the area Minsc is.

Facing Minsc[edit section | visual editor]

Once getting close to Minsc, a cutscene plays, showing  Minsc and a  Jaheira doppelganger giving gold to a Zhentarim agent - either  Roah Moonglow or  Boss Friol, if Roah is dead. After finding Minsc again, he does not see reason, as his tadpole is controlling him. He immediately attacks the party, along with a number of doppelgangers and cultists. Meanwhile, Roah (or Friol) immediately flees to take on the Guildhall. The party must defeat the cultists and knock out Minsc using non-lethal attacks. Once all enemies have been defeated, he rises up and panics at the loss of a doppelganger, who had transformed into Jaheira to control him.

 The Emperor initially refuses to bring Minsc under his protection, stating that he is too unpredictable to help. However, he can be convinced to bring him under his protection. Jaheira must have been recruited before to protect Minsc. If Jaheira went alone or if she dies, then Minsc cannot be recruited.

If Minsc is successfully freed from the Absolute's control, then he runs off to find his friend  Boo by smashing into several walls. If Minsc dies, Boo spawns next to him although he cannot be interacted with.

Returning to the Guildhall[edit section | visual editor]

If they survived the previous battle, Roah (or Friol) approach the party upon returning to the Guildhall. If Roah (or Friol) or Minsc died in the previous battle, then the Zhentarim are automatically hostile and attack the party and the Guild. Otherwise, they propose an alliance - the Zhentarim are prepared to take down the Guild and reign as the new underlords of the city. The party can either accept an alliance with them or attack them and side with the Guild. If the Zhentarim have already been killed, Roah (or Friol) is surprised and cancels the takeover, as there are not enough agents to take over the Guild.

If the supported faction leader survives the ensuing fight, they agree to support the party with every blade they have in the fight against the Absolute. If Roah (or Friol) was sided with, the party can tell her that they intend to take the Guild for themselves, causing another fight. Speaking to  Nine-Fingers Keene as Minsc after the conflict has been resolved in the Guild's favor results in a conversation where it is necessary to convince Minsc not to start a fight.

Quest Rewards[edit section | visual editor]

  • ⁠Cleaning up the Scum ( Haunted One background) - for killing both groups on the Western Beach
  • ⁠Easy Pickings ( Criminal background) - for killing both groups on the Western Beach
  • ⁠The Wealth of Morality ( Noble backgrounds) - for picking either side in the conflict

The Guild supported[edit section | visual editor]

  • Support of the Guild in the final fight, as allies using the ability  Guildmaster Keene's Fixers for ⁠Gather Your Allies
  • ⁠Backing the Established Players ( Noble) background) - for siding with Farlin on the Western Beach
  • ⁠Honour Among Thieves ( Criminal backgrounds) - for siding with the Guild in the fight in the Guildhall
  • ⁠Woman of Her Word ( Charlatan backgrounds) - for siding with the Guild in the fight in the Guildhall

Zhentarim supported[edit section | visual editor]

  • Support of the Zhentarim in the final fight, as allies using the ability  Black Fist Enforcers for ⁠Gather Your Allies
  • ⁠It's Just Business ( Criminal backgrounds) - for siding with the Zhentarim in the fight in the Guildhall
  • ⁠Winged Serpent of Gold ( Charlatan backgrounds) - for siding with the Zhentarim in the fight in the Guildhall
